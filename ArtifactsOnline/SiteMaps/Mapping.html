Site Layout, Maps and Interpretation
Archaeologists create and use maps for a wide variety of on and off site purposes.  At the outset, maps are used to locate archaeological sites and remains within regions and at the intrasite level of analysis.  In other words, while maps are used to locate the relative position of a site, they are also used to identify and locate the positions of artifacts and features with relationship to one another.  The following map icons provide some sense of the way in which maps are interpreted.  Click on the icons to see larger versions of these same images.  Photo: San Juan Bautista site notebook, including a mapping of the artifacts and features within a single excavation unit.
