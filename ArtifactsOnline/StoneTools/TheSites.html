Phase Three: Interpreting the Activity Areas

Perhaps the most difficult aspect of any archaeological undertaking is the interpretation of the many and varied forms of data that result from such research.  Having sampled the debris matrix created for this purpose, you are now in a position to interpret the results of the data that you have collected.

Tabled data should provide some indication of the nature and extent of the original activities undertaken at the sites sampled.  The relative size of the materials, particularly the length of flake tools and debitage produced, should provide indications of the type of core reduction activities that took place at the site or sites sampled.
