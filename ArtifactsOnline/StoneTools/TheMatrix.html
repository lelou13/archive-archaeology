Phase Two: The Sampling of the Debris Field

Having created a series of debris fields or activity areas as a result of the reduction of obsidian cores, the next phase of this activity is to determine how you are going to effectively sample the area in question.  Because of the number of activity areas that are evident, you should choose a sampling strategy that will allow you to determine the sorts of "core reduction" or related activities that took place at this site.

Will you use a random sampling strategy?  A stratified random sampling strategy?  Or, will you sample only from known activity areas visible on the matrix?

Having decided on a sampling strategy, you must now determine a classification strategy that will allow you to differentiate different types of materials to be collected.  Will you base your classifications on the actual weights, or, with respect to relative size measurements of the flakes collected?  Bear in mind that your classifications will affect the representation and visualization of quantities and types as per your database charts and tables.
