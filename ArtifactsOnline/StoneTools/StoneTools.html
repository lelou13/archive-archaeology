Stone Tool Identification
Stone tools pose a particular challenge when it comes to questions of types and typologies.  The following artifact images and terms provide one basis for properly describing the characteristics of stone tool technologies.  The examples presented in this preliminary version of the Stone Tools technology page of this site are types typical of the Archaic and Paleo-Indian periods of High Plains (Colorado and Nebraska) prehistory.

In this particular example of a Plains Indian bifacial tool, both sides of the finely worked tool have been crafted by way of "pressure flaking."  Because this tool has roughly parallel sides, it is typically referred to as "lanceolate" in form.  The "preform" that anticipated the creation of this biface was very likely prepared with a combination of direct and indirect percussion flaking techniques using antler billets and flaking tools.
