Here at the Institute of Archaeology, we are working to scan the better part of the archaeological collection of Old Mission San Juan Bautista.  Our goal is to create an image database of any and all archaeologically-recovered materials from said site.  Once posted, the database will serve as a resource for scholars and students studying the material culture of the California missions.  Note: You may click on any of the above four images to view a "JPEG" version of the size and content of each image scan required of any and all artifacts entered into the image database.  Artifact has been identified as a "Purple Transfer-print on white slip earthenware shard."

Scanning Artifacts for the Database

Precautionary Note: Given that flatbed scanners were not originally intended to scan objects in the round...or for that matter, objects with the dust and grime of centuries, it is important to remember that the scanning of artifacts must be done with great care so as to insure that one's scanner is not damaged.  As such, you should take care to avoid allowing the accumulation of dust on the glass scanning bed.  Carefully remove any and all dust at regular intervals so as to avoid damage to the scanning surface.  In addition, you should wear cotton gloves so as to avoid leaving finger prints (and their attendant oils) on the scanning surface.  While not injurious to the scanning surface, the fingerprints will appear in final scanned images if you are not careful.

     1.  Use the following alphanumeric (letter and number-based) format for labeling scanned PSD’s and JPG’s: sjb00819-00-1 (where sjb = San Juan Bautista; 00819 = main catalog number of five digits; 00 = the secondary catalog number count; and the 1 represents the scan number for both the Photoshop Document file (.psd) and the Jpeg (.jpg) files.

     2.  Where possible, the artifact background should be black (in the case of light or white colored artifacts), and white (in the case of very dark or black colored artifacts).

     3.  Scanner settings on the Agfa Duoscan T-1200 or T-2400 are as follows:  Initial scan (with metric measure included) should be done at 150 pixels/inch, as a Reflective scan, in RGB color, at 100%.  Width of final image should be set at 4.0 inches, height at 4.0 (or 3.795 inches), at a resolution of 72 pixels/inch.  File size at this rate is about 231K.  Initial image should be a PSD file, and the second image should be a Jpeg file (at high quality or at a setting of 6).

     4.  Initial scan with metric measure should include all 10 centimeters of the metric measure placed just below the artifacts being scanned.  You should place the artifact(s) at the center of a 10 cm. X 10 cm. Frame (ca. 4” x 4”).

     5.  Where artifacts, such as porcelains or other ceramics, include patterns, designs, or other distinctive features, scans should be done of each side of the artifact.  The same main/secondary catalog number should be retained for the other side of the artifact, but the final digit, included after the catalog number proper, will change one increment (e.g., sjb00819-00-2 to sjb00819-00-3).

     6.  In the cropped scan of the artifact, without the metric measure, the number will change similarly (e.g., sjb00819-00-1 to sjb00819-00-2).  In addition, the scan settings (Photoshop) for the artifact without the metric measure will be as follows: width = 2 inches; height = 2 inches; resolution = 150 pixels/inch.

     7.  In sum, if the artifact being scanned has distinctive features on both its top and  bottom face, then you should produce the following scans of said artifact at the settings noted above.

          (a)  Top of artifact with 10 cm scale at bottom edge of artifact in PSD (Photoshop) format for archive,

          (b)  Top of artifact with 10 cm scale at bottom edge of artifact in JPEG format for posting to Internet,

          (c)  Top of artifact without 10 cm scale cropped to artifact in any rectangular crop that fully incorporates the body of the artifact saved in PSD format,

          (d)  Top of artifact without 10 cm scale cropped to artifact in any rectangular crop that fully incorporates the body of the artifact saved in JPEG format,

          (e)  Bottom side of artifact with 10 cm scale at bottom edge of artifact in PSD (Photoshop) format for archive,

          (f)  Bottom of artifact with 10 cm scale at bottom edge of artifact in JPEG format for posting to Internet,

          (g)  Bottom of artifact without 10 cm scale cropped to artifact in any rectangular crop that fully incorporates the body of the artifact saved in PSD format, and

          (h)  Bottom of artifact without 10 cm scale cropped to artifact in any rectangular crop that fully incorporates the body of the artifact saved in JPEG format.  In this instance, you should have eight scanned images (four in PSD format and four in JPEG format).

     8.   All images scanned on a particular day should be saved in a desktop folder (or on Zip disk) with the Folder titled in the following format: “sjbArtifacts8-25-99” (where sjb = San Juan Bautista, Artifacts = subject, and 8-25-99 = date of scanning).
