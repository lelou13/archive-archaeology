Core Readings List
The following readings are exemplary of the range of core readings used in those undergraduate courses offered by the Institute of Archaeology.  So as to prepare for your undergraduate capstone project and annotated readings list, archaeology students should consult with the Institute Director for guidance on which of the following resources would be most applicable to their respective course of study.  Graduate students will be directed to assemble a readings list specific to their research emphasis.
Primary Readings: Archaeology

Ayres, James E. (ed.) 1995. The Archaeology of Spanish and Mexican Colonialism in the American Southwest. Guides to the Archaeological Literature of the Immigrant Experience in America, Number 3. Ann Arbor, Michigan: The Society for Historical Archaeology.

Barber, Russell J. 1994. Doing Historical Archaeology: Exercises Using Documentary, Oral, and Material Evidence. Upper Saddle River, New Jersey: Prentice Hall.

Binford, L. R. 1987. "Data, Relativism, and Archaeological Science." Man 22: 391404.

Butzer, Karl W. 1982. Archaeology as Human Ecology.  New York: Cambridge University Press.

Conkey, M. W., and C. Hastorf (eds.). 1990. The Uses of Style in Archaeology. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press.

Jameson, Jr., John H. (ed.). 1997. Presenting Archaeology to the Public: Digging for Truths. Walnut Creek: Altamira Press.

Lamberg?Karlovsky, C. C. (ed.). 1989. Archaeological Thought in America.  New York: Cambridge University Press.
