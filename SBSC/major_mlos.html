Major Learning Outcomes (MLO's)
All SBSC students are expected to obtain a thorough understanding of the major and its underlying intellectual rationale by taking a series of required courses and obtaining training in the areas of the three MLO's. Competency is required in all three areas.
 
Area 1: Theory
    (Area 1 is met through the successful completion of required courses, SBSC 300A/B)
The competency area of Theory promotes an understanding of classic and contemporary theories of society, the individual, cultures, and economies. Students must be able to apply integrative theoretical frameworks to explain social phenomena, especially how various aspects of social sciences (such as individual and group behaviors, socio-economic issues cultural and political dynamics, issues related to the analysis of gender, ethnic groups, communities, etc.), are influenced by geographical and historical processes. They must demonstrate this knowledge in their learning experience and projects.
  • Area 2: Research Methodology, Data Analysis and Management
    • (Area 2 qualitative data collection is met through the successful completion of required courses, SBSC 310A/B. Area 2 quantitative data collection can be met through an approved statistics course or SBSC 211/311)
The competency area of research methodology, data analysis and management addresses the need to apply standard quantitative and qualitative data collection and analysis methods to social and behavioral science issues. This requires that students learn to use modern electronic technologies to collect, manage and retrieve social, cultural, economic, historical, geographical, political, and psychological data.
  • Area 3: Specialized competencies: Disciplinary and Area Studies
    • (Area 3 is met through the successful compeletion of the Senior Capstone Experience)
     
    The main objective is to provide students with an opportunity to make a smooth transition in terms of preparation for graduate studies or professional training in traditional universities (those that do not have an interdisciplinary instructional focus). This area is evaluated based the assessment of a senior portfolio that contains evidence of a student's mastery of Area III. Learning in-depth skills within the social sciences or in areas of professional activity can be gained in the larger society, other countries, or the local communities. The area studies specialization helps students gain knowledge about diverse cultures in the U.S. and of cultures outside the U.S. Moreover, the disciplinary specialization provides students with a foundation in traditional disciplines including anthropology, archaeology, cultural geography, social history, political science, economics, psychology, and sociology. This specialization, which may begin in the project based learning labs, is formalized in the year-long senior capstone learning experience. As an accompaniment to the senior capstone experience, there is a directed reading lab designed to give students an exposure to both classic and contemporary theoretical frameworks from a given discipline. Thus, disciplinary specialization and expertise is obtained by SBS majors through specific learning experiences in the major. Students may also propose (via the Individual Learning Plan) study in other centers and majors that may strengthen their preparation. This makes it feasible for our majors to focus on interdisciplinary training, while also obtaining prerequisite preparation for entering into graduate programs in universities with traditional disciplinary tracks.
