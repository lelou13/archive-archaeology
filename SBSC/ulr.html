We have established learning requirements differently at CSUMB than you may be used to. Rather than graduating based on which courses you took, you will graduate based on what you have learned. Our system works a little bit like getting your driver's license. To get a driver's license, you need to demonstrate that you know how to drive and that you know the rules of the road. You can learn these things in a variety of ways, for example, taking private lessons, doing trial and error, or some combination. When you do get your license, you are not held accountable for how you learned to drive, but rather for demonstrating that you are able to drive. The University Learning Requirements are listed here.


The ULR System

How Can Students Satisfy the ULRs ?

First, a student should decide which ULR System best meets her or his needs. Then, for each ULR within that set, a student basically has three (3) choices:

1. If a student does not already possess the knowledge and skills required by a ULR, then the student can register for one of the courses which have formally been designated as offering preparation and assessment for that ULR, and the student will be assessed by the instructor(s) right in the course.

2. If the student does not already possess the required knowledge and skills, but he or she has a plan to gain the required knowledge in some way other than taking one of the designated courses, then the student should work out that plan with her or his advisor. After the student has gained the required knowledge through this alternative means, then the student would register for an independent assessment of that ULR.

3. If the student believes that he or she already possess the knowledge and skills contained in a particular ULR, then the student should register for the appropriate independent assessment (ASMT) for that ULR. After registering for the ASMT section corresponding to the ULR, then the student should see the chair of that ULR Committee for instructions on how to prepare for the independent assessment.


Goals

Effective and ethical communication in at least two languages with widely diverse audiences

This goal entails being able to identify your audience, including their concerns, viewpoints, and primary language; use a means of expression that best communicates to the audience as well as fits the message you wish to communicate; experience communication as a two-way process in which you understand what others are saying to you as well as communicate your own ideas effectively; demonstrate versatility in the language and mode of communication you are able to use, including oral and written modes; use technology appropriately as a tool to enhance the communication process; demonstrate a sense of ethics of your use of communication; and practice effective communication in collaborative work.

Cross-culturally competent citizenship in a pluralistic and global society

California provides a microcosm of the fascinating range of cultural diversity that makes up the U.S.; and the U.S. is only one of over 100 nations and hundreds of cultural traditions on the globe. We are interdependent, whether as individuals we choose to be or not. CSUMB believes that this diversity is an exciting and valuable human resource. Our graduates should have the skills, knowledge, and dispositions to establish respectful and constructive relationships with people who differ from themselves, understand how human differences become fault lines for conflict, and participate actively, constructively, and ethically in a culturally diverse society. This entails having a deep knowledge of the complexity and dynamic nature of culture, one¹s own culture, and at least one or two cultures that differ from one¹s own. It entails having skills of learning in cross-cultural contexts, and having formed personal relationships with people who differ culturally from oneself. It also entails having a sense of contemporary issues that relate to cross-cultural dynamics, and to various ways in which those issues are expressed.

Technological, aural, and visual literacy

Students will leave CSUMB equipped to participate effectively and thoughtfully in the information explosion of the 21 century. This means that students will become proficient at using the technological capabilities of settings around them, to access information, solve problems, create products, and communicate. Students will become adept at seeking out varied form sand sources information, evaluating it, and using it. In addition, students will develop the capacity to read the media around them critically, understanding what messages are being conveyed, as well as the viewpoints, beliefs, and assumption sin and absent from those messages.

Creative expression in the service of transforming culture

This goal is based on the fundamental premise that creating Art is a significant social act, and that the education of artists, be they painters, musicians, dancers, actors, etc. must include an awareness of their potential to connect with a broad audience and the public agenda. Understanding cultural difference and multiple points of view is the cornerstone of our approach to both history and contemporary issues. There is a social responsibility in making culture, and the faculty at CSUMB is committed to working with students in the spirit of free inquiry to help them recognize for themselves the implications in that responsibility.

Ethics, social justice, and care for one another

This goal entails being able to recognize the complexity and importance of choices available to humans in their personal, professional and social lives; conscious awareness of deeply held beliefs about what is important, right, wrong, good, and bad that affect how we relate to ourselves and others in specific contexts; the ability to recognize and understand others approaches to ethical decision making; and the ability to participate meaningfully and successfully in dialogues across these differences.
