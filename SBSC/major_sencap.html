Senior Capstone Experience

Philosophy and Learning Outcomes

Capstone Philosophy

The SBSC Senior Capstone is a highly individualized and intensive year-long learning experience. During the year-long experience students carry out approved projects and/or research activities. The activities in each student's proposal is customized to meet individual educational and career goals. In addition, the activities of the Capstone are augmented with complimentary readings from their Senior Reading Lab (SBSC 401).
Explicit goals or learning outcomes are provided for the students based on their proposal. (See below.) Aside from the explicit goals, the nature of the Capstone activity provides the opportunity for students to gain process-oriented skills. By successfully completing their Senior Capstone students will not just have acquired explicit knowledge about their field of study, but will have implicitly gained necessary real-life skills. Some of these skills include problem-solving, critical analysis, logical thinking, time management, and resourcefulness.

Learning Outcomes (as applicable to individual proposals)

1. Ability to formulate a theoretical problem in a researchable fashion

2. Ability to address real life problems using theories from the social and behavioral sciences

3. Ability to generate a database and analyze contents in light of appropriate area within the social and behavioral sciences

4. Ability to generate a database and analyze contents in light of a real life problem pertaining to the social and behavioral sciences

5. Ability to integrate experiential activity with appropriate theories in the social and behavioral sciences

6. Ability to conduct advanced critical analyses in a specific field in the social and behavioral sciences for the purpose of continuing on to an advanced degree

Demonstration of Learning Outcomes

Students will be expected to submit a final comprehensive deliverable (paper, video, CD-ROM etc.) detailing the results of their theoretical and empirical and/or applied research. This deliverable will in turn be the subject-matter for the oral component of the graduation process. In addition to the final paper, students participating in internships will be required to submit a site evaluation form and a supervisor evaluation form. The culmination of products resulting from the Capstone experience will be organized into a graduation portfolio. Aside from the final capstone project, the contents of the portfolio will include items demonstrating competency in the SBS major's three learning outcomes. (See graduation portfolio guidelines for details.) Lastly, the student will be required to come before their committee and present the results of their year-long Capstone project. This typically takes place in May during the University-wide Capstone Festival. Students graduating in December will need to make alternate arrangements with their committee.
