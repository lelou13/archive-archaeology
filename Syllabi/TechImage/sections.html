Section Descriptions

SBSC 395: Section 1A (2 hours): Spans that body of theory and method specific to the area of visual anthropology and documentary photography. While hands-on field and lab exercises will be introduced in this section, primary emphasis will be with introducing students to the world of investigative, documentary, and forensic photography as well as that body of social science information that can be derived from the interpretation of both modern and historic photographic images. Field exercises will promote the development of basic technical proficiencies in the use of the 35mm Single-Lens-Reflex or SLR camera and related equipment. As such, all students are required to obtain and use a 35mm single-lens-reflex (or larger format) camera for all lab and field exercises. CSUMB Learning Outcomes: ARTSCOM, CP, CULT, ENGCOM, EQUITY, INFO, MEDIA, TECH. SBSC Learning Outcomes: Area 1: Theory and Method; Area 2: Information Management and Data Retrieval; Area 3: Specialized Competencies. Offered Fall 1996.

SBSC 395: Section 1B (2 or 4 hours): This section is intended to advance each students respective lab and field experience with photo-technical imaging applications in the social sciences. Readings, lab and field exercises include the hands-on application and experimentation with methods used in the social sciences, and particularly those used in archaeology, anthropology, criminology, forensics, and museum studies. Lab and field exercises will center on facilitating and coordinating the production of student portfolios that include a body of work reflecting the study of one or more communities or themes drawn from within the social and behavioral sciences. CSUMB Learning Outcomes: ARTSCOM, CP, CULT, ENGCOM, EQUITY, INFO, MEDIA, TECH. SBSC Learning Outcomes: Area 1: Theory and Method; Area 2: Information Management and Data Retrieval; Area 3: Specialized Competencies. Offered Spring 1997.
