Primary Learning Requirements


Students will be introduced to both fundamental and advanced techniques in the photographic and technical processes underlying the use of technical imaging in the social sciences. Exercises will stress a variety of fundamental skills aimed at the development of a photographic portfolio that bridges the art and science of photographic methods as applied in social science contexts.
Each student will be required to obtain and learn the fundamental skills underlying the use of the 35mm camera. A photographic portfolio will be the primary requirement of this learning experience, whereas, a photographic lab and field journal will be the second major requirement. Because assigned readings will serve as the basis for your introduction and advancement in technical imaging skills development, you are expected to provide documentation that you have in fact completed all assigned readings. This may be done by way of your photographic journal. Because this is a lab and field-based learning experience, your virtual and actual participation will serve as the third primary measure of assessment. Primary assessment of your learning experience will be by way of scheduled interviews with your professor at midterm and end-term. Individual photographic assignments will be submitted and evaluated on a weekly, or as needed, basis for evidence of your attainment of basic technical proficiencies and the appropriateness and quality of specific images intended for inclusion in the final photographic portfolio. Please note that all final portfolios for this learning lab should follow the appropriate format for that specified for the photographic portfolio, and should not include extraneous materials that have not been specifically requested.

Precautions for Lab and Field Exercises

Due to the nature of the lab and field projects that will be undertaken in this learning experience, it may be necessary from time to time to wear protection (i.e. rubber, cotton, or leather gloves, helmets, lab coats, protective goggles, respirators, etc.) and use tongs or other appropriate tools for the handling of materials. In no instance should you risk endangering yourself or other members of the team by engaging in actions that create unnecessary risk in the lab or field -- such as those risks resulting from the careless handling of chemicals, equipment, and artifacts. Where lab and field exercises are concerned, you should be prepared to wear protective eye gear, rubber or cloth gloves, and respirators where necessary and or appropriate. For those that have difficulty with heat, cold or humidity, you should take the necessary precautions to avoid heat exhaustion, dehydration, or other stressors when engaged in field exercises.

Liability Release and Waiver (Hold Harmless): A Liability Release and Waiver or Hold Harmless form is required of all students participating in this particular lab and field experience. While this is a formality, it does provide us with documentation that you have been informed of the risks of participating in the lab and field exercises of this lab and field-based learning experience.

Multimedia Release and Waiver Form: Because photographs, video, and other forms of multimedia recording may be used to document lab and field exercises, you are required to sign a Multimedia Release and Waiver Form so that said materials may be used by the instructor, staff, and students of this institution in project-based learning experiences and projects that entail multimedia development and production. See the form for further details regarding options to the rule.
