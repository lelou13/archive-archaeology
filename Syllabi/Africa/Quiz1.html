    1) In Sudanic Africa, trade 
    A) constantly competed with agriculture as a way of life.

    B) was viewed with suspicion by warrior elites.

    C) was an integral part of political, social, and economic life.

    D) meant that merchants would be more powerful than kings.

    2) The desert-edge zone just to the north and south of the Sahara was known as the 
    A) Sahel.

    B) Fezzan.

    C) Hoggar massif.

    D) savanna.

    3) Which of the following statements about trade in Africa is most accurate? 
    A) The existence of successful trading networks depended directly on agricultural productivity.

    B) Most parts of the sub-Saharan Sudanic belt could produce more or less the same commodities.

    C) Trading networks began to be established only around A.D. 500.

    D) Trade was encouraged by natural demand for different commodities between environmentally different regions.

    4) In trans-Saharan commerce, which of the following metals was probably not as important a trade item as the others listed below? 
    A) gold

    B) silver

    C) copper

    D) iron

    5) Around ca. A.D. 1100, trade began to work to the advantage of states forming in the 
    A) Sahel.

    B) savanna south of the desert.

    C) Sahara.

    D) west African forest zones.

    6) Within trading networks, 
    A) local exchange and long-distance trade were closely connected and upheld each other.

    B) local exchange was confined to temporary market-places which served only regional needs.

    C) long-distance traders remained self-sufficient by bringing with them food and other supplies necessary for the journey.

    D) market-places tended to remain outside and independent of cities.

    7) In the world of the "desert-edge," 
    A) war posed a constant threat to economic stability.

    B) jihads were carried out only for religious reasons.

    C) Berber nomads often united to cripple Sudanic states such as Ghana.

    D) frequent armed conflict was a means of regulating economic activity.

    8) The "ideology of protection" operating in Saharan and Sudanic regions meant that 
    A) kings directly protected merchants from warrior raids.

    B) clerics enjoyed special tax exemptions.

    C) warrior elites defended traders from harassment by other fighters.

    D) merchants sought clerical approval as a means of guaranteeing divine protection for themselves.

    9) The ___________ were the chief group of peoples controlling the trans-Saharan trade. 
    A) Berbers.

    B) Soninke.

    C) Arabs.

    D) Kanuri.

    10) The biggest rival trading networks across the Sahara were based in 
    A) the two transitional zones just to the north and south of the Sahara.

    B) the north African coastal cities vs. Timbuktu and other desert-edge cities.

    C) two north-south networks stretching from Morocco to the Niger and from Cairo to the central Sudan.

    D) Berber oasis settlements vs. expanding savanna states.
