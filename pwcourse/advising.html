Online Advising and Questions

The Seal Server: You may e-mail your questions and requests for advising via the FirstClass Instranet student e-mail accounts available to you. The "SBSC Classes" (e.g., SBSC 348) "drop folder" located on the "Seal Server" provides an on-line location for depositing assignments and deliverables, and the SBSC Peer Counselors are available at Building 17 for your assistance.

Important Notice: When submitting deliverables and other assignments via the internet, please provide a clear and complete identification label for your submissions. For example: Joan Doe, who is registered in the lower division portion of the Fall 2001 SBSC 348 class, and who is submitting Major Deliverable 2  (in MacIntosh computer format), might title her e-mail submission and attachment as follows: sbsc348.doe.F98.md2 (upper or lower case letters are acceptable).  Anyone using a Pentium or IBM-clone may abbreviate to 348m2doe.doc.  Any assignment that does not contain the title and or  format in question will not be accepted for evaluation.

All electronic submissions should be dropped into the "Class Folder" specific to the section within which you are enrolled: i.e., SBSC 248 submissions should be dropped in the SBSC 248 Classes Folder, whereas SBSC 348 students will submit their assignments in the SBSC 348 Classes Folder.

Questions:  Please feel free to ask questions or request assistance from your professor during or after class, or from any of the Peer Counselors available in Building 17.
