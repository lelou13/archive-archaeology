Lab Deliverable 8

Maya Photo Exhibit Summary


Description: Anthropologists and other social scientists are often faced with the daunting task of interpreting a cultural scene, setting, or event. The purpose of tonight's exercise is to have you interpret one person's construction of a cultural reality. Given that your professor currently has an exhibit on the subject of the Maya in Building 49, we will be heading to building 49 (World Languages and Cultures on "A" Street and Sixth Avenue) between 5:45 and 6:00 p.m. this evening.

 

1. Break into your pre-assigned group or team. If you do not have a team designation, or are included within a group too small to collaborate, please join the members of the next smallest team.

2. Having come together, each team member should be assigned a key role in interpreting one specific image from the collection or exhibit in question. Because this should be a collaborative assignment, each team member should have an assigned task (e.g. interpretation, notetaking, translation, etc.).

3. Whereas your individual interpretations should hinge on the analysis of a specific image and its content, your group interpretation should combine each individual appraisal into one larger interpretation of the theme, its content, and cultural message.

4. Prior to reconvening for the purposes of discussing the individual images in a group context, each team member should prepare a one paragraph description of what they believe the specific photographic image or artifact has to say about the Maya in general, and this exhibit in particular. You should be prepared to submit this proposed individual interpretation at the end of today's class.

5. In addition to preparing the individual interpretation and description of your chosen image, your group should in addition prepare one thematic statement centered on the overall content of the larger collection of images and or artifacts. This statement should center on an overall impression of the Maya based on the content and subjectmatter of the representations in question. Bear in mind that this is but one view of the Maya based on one photographer's vision of a people...both past and present.
