SBSC 100A

Theory and Method in the Social and Behavioral Sciences

Texts

Rethinking History

Jenkins, Keith 1991. New York: Routledge. [77 pages]

This book examines the methodological as opposed to skills-based approach to history. Draws from the areas of philosophy, literary theory, critical theory and politics in an effort to develop in its readers a "historicist, skeptical, critical intelligence that will open up a new understanding of what history is, and can be, in the post-modern world"

Cultural Ecology. Second Edition.

Netting, Robert M. 1986. Second Edition. Prospect Height, Illinois:Waveland Press.

This book provides an overview of the potential contributions and pitfalls inherent in using those perspectives drawn from cultural ecology and allied perspectives. Explores the role of environment and localized ecologies and adaptive strategies in several human societies

Note: Selected chapter readings will be assigned from the following texts that have been placed on reserve at the Reserve Desk of the Library Learning Complex.

1995 Annual Editions: Archaeology 95/96

Hasten, Linda L. (ed.) Sluice Dock, Guilford, Connecticut: The Dushkin Publishing Group.

A collection of readings drawn from the field of archaeology and allied areas of study. Readings selected are those that pertain to the broader question of how and why social scientists, and specifically archaeologists, draw the inferences and pursue the questions that they do

The Cultural Landscape: An Introduction to Human Geography. Fifth Edition.

Rubenstein, James M. 1996 Upper Saddle River, New Jersey: Prentice Hall.

This work provides an overview and general introduction to world cultural geography and demography from the perspective of those cultural and geographic perspectives brought to bear in recent years within the field of cultural geography

Migrations in Prehistory: Inferring Population Movement from Cultural Remains

Rouse, Irving 1986 New Haven: Yale University Press.

Examines the archaeological evidence for prehistoric and historic population movements and migrations in such diverse groups as those represented by the Polynesians, Eskimos, Japanese, and Tainos

Gender and Migration in Developing Countries

Sylvia Chant (ed.) 1992. New York: Belhaven Press

Becoming a Master Student. Seventh Edition

Ellis, Dave 1994 Boston, Mass: Houghton Mifflin Company.
