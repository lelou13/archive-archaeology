Archaeology: From Map to Museum

Lab Deliverable 1: Archaeology Video Review
Directions:  You will review the video "Myths and Moundbuilders" (Odyssey, 1988) and will produce notes relevant to three primary issues discussed in the video.  You should be prepared to include your video notes in your final portfolio, and your responses to the following three questions should be E-mailed to Professor Mendoza at astv@monterey.ml.org (click below or enter address via Firstclass Intranet) by the next class period.

Questions:  Please provide your response to the following three questions.  Your response should reflect your understanding of the three issues based on class discussion and video-related notes produced during the viewing.

1.    Identify the primary "myths" of archaeology and the moundbuilders as noted in the video commentary.  Having first identified the principal myths noted, please discuss what you feel serves to explain the origins of the myths in question?

2.    What value would you ascribe to the "mound reconstruction" experiment undertaken by the archaeologists portrayed in the video?  Why was the experiment important, and what did the experiment seek to demonstrate?

3.    Reflect on the value and approach represented by the so-called "salvage archaeology" approach portrayed in the video.  Please bear in mind that several of the archaeology projects depicted in the video were undertaken so as to "salvage" the remains of sites under threat of destruction by development or public works projects.  Based on what you observed, and indicated in your notes, what is "salvage archaeology" and how is it beneficial (and or destructive) to the interpretation of the prehistory of North America?
