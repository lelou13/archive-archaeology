What is this course about?

ProSeminar 100 provides the foundations needed for academic life at CSUMB and for life-long learning. It is a pathway to meeting the developmental standards required by the CSU System and CSUMB in reading comprehension, written and oral communication, and critical listening and thinking. You will participate in learning experiences designed to develop or refine your basic information literacy skills and cross-cultural awareness. You will have rich opportunities to develop your ability to engage meaningfully in dialogue with people whose values, experiences, beliefs, and interests differ from your own, and to enhance your ability to collaborate with others in decision-making, learning and completing academic and/or creative activities. You will learn a great deal about yourself as an individual, community member, and a cultural and global being.

To guide you academically beyond ProSeminar 100, you will develop a "Learning Educational Plan," identifying the knowledge, skills and abilities required to meet your learning needs at CSUMB, as well as your personal, social and professional goals. The end result of ProSeminar 100 will be a portfolio which contains your self-assessment and documentation of projects completed in ProSeminar 100, along with your Individual Learning Plan (ILP).

SBSC Special Interest Group (SIG) Sections
These special interest groups are designed for entry level students who think they may be interested in the Social and Behavioral Sciences and would like to know more about the SBS major here at CSUMB. However, students who do not expect to become SBS majors are also welcome. Formerly known as SBSC 100 A and B, Introduction to Social and Behavioral Sciences. No prerequisites.
