The Individualized Learning Plan or ILP

The Petition to Major includes the development of an Individual Learning Plan (ILP). This plan defines students' learning outcomes and explains how these learning outcomes will be achieved; either by pathways (i.e., course works), field experiences, service learning, or prior experiences in the workplace or in life. In particular, it describes what the student has done and will do in order to become competent in the three Major Learning Outcomes required for the SBS degree. Each ILP contains both required and optional learning experiences.

The ILP features learning pathways and learning experiences that help students assess, with guidance from faculty and staff, their own progress toward graduation and toward meeting their professional and career goals. This Individual Learning Plan is essentially a contract for graduation once approved. Lower level students at CSUMB are introduced to the idea of outcome based learning through the SBSC introductory experiences; they may begin their ILP in these courses (SBSC 100A and 100B). For upper- level students (junior and senior transfer students), the ILP is authored during their first semester SBSC 308 Professional Assessment Lab.

A final Assessment Portfolio is submitted shortly before graduation. This portfolio is assessed to evaluate the achievement of the SBS learning outcomes. Such a portfolio may also provide support for the achievement of CSUMB University Learning Requirements. The portfolio may take the form of hard copy and multimedia or electronic (HTML or CD-ROM) documents that will become the student's permanent record of achievement within the Social and Behavioral Sciences Center at CSUMB.

This assessment process is directed by the assessment professional. The students may request the assistance of other CSUMB faculty members in a review of the ILP. This assessment will include a review of any traditional (alphanumeric) grades assigned to their courses, as well as a review of their performances as articulated in the ILPs. It will be at this time that the assessment professional may schedule consultations with other faculty and the student to assess progress toward achieving the competencies. The results of this review will become part of each student's senior graduation portfolio and may be included in an updated ILP.
