Course Description and Outcomes

SBSC 308A/B Requirements: The SBSC Professional Assessment Labs (308A & B) must be taken by all Social and Behavioral Science majors concurrently enrolled in SBSC 300A/B: Theory and Method in the Social and Behavioral Sciences. As of the Spring of 1998, the SBSC 310: Project-Based Learning Lab will no longer be required.

Major Learning Outcomes (MLO) : This learning experience addresses the AREA II: Information Retrieval and Analysis MLO for the BA degree in the Social and Behavioral Sciences.

Focus and Goals:This Professional Assessment Lab is intended only for those students who wish to petition to major in the Social and Behavioral Sciences. The Petition to Major is considered complete when students have developed an acceptable draft of their Individual Learning Plan (for first semester majors) and when an acceptable proposal has been written for the Senior Capstone Project (second semester majors).

Learning Goals:
Learn the elementary requisites for being a successful professional, especially in the social and behavioral sciences.

Guide students through the preparation of the documentation (deliverables) needed for petitioning to become an SBSC major.

Guide students through the preparation of the documentation (deliverables) needed in all core deliverables.

Introduce students not going to graduate school, to the elementary baseline of information needed to plan a career in, or which utilizes knowledge and skills derived from the social and behavioral sciences.

Develop strategies for students to select and successfully apply for graduate and professional training programs.

Develop strategies for learning about GRE exams and graduate fellowship opportunities.

Sharpen writing and information presentation, including oral presentation, skills in the social and behavioral sciences.

Learn professional and institutional networking skills for reaching career and educational goals.

Further develop the capacity for collegial sharing and support as a professional, while providing responsible, temperate, fair and helpful or professional critiques of others.

Analyze the logic and style of writing in the social sciences and the manner of writing scientific papers as per established and widely used cannons in the social sciences.

Develop the electronic communication skills necessary for success in the SBSC program.
