Chronology and Its Basis
Chronology: Based on both vertical (temporal) and horizontal (regional) considerations.
Maya Classic: Period from A.D. 300-900 was pivotal to defining “Pre-, Classic, Post-” periods.
Regional Variants: Formative, Protoclassic, Middle Classic, Epiclassic…
Secular Chronology: BCE = Before Common Era, CE = Common Era (compare BC & AD); or, C14: BP = Before Present (I.e., Before A.D. 1950), Radiocarbon, Maya glyphs, thermoluminescence, obsidian hydration…

Chronology Methods
Ethnohistoric Resources (Prime): Contact period chronicles and codices used for interpretation.
Archaeological Research: (Prime) Excavation and study of archaeological sites and artifacts.
Absolute vs Relative Dating: Absolute = Radiocarbon (C14), Thermoluminescence, Dendrochronology, Obsidian Hydration; Relative = Ceramics, Art Styles, Architecture, etc….Maya glyphs occupy a middle ground in this scheme…though primarily absolute.

The Chronology

Variations on Chronology
Formative: Identifies the early development of civilization with Olmec and contemporaries.
Protoclassic: Used to account for early and advanced or precocious  developments (Maya).
Middle Classic: Pasztory’s “Horizon” of Teotihuacan influence.
Epiclassic: Similar to Terminal Classic; Equated with period of internecine warfare and urban collapse.

Chronological Periods
Paleoindian (9500-7000 BCE): Earliest Native American settlements; Big Game Hunters.
Archaic (7000-2500 BCE): Agricultural origins and early villages.
Early Preclassic (2500-900 BCE): Pox-Purron and Ocos ceramic traditions; Olmec origins.
Middle Preclassic (900-300 BCE): Height of Olmec civilization and influence in Mesoamerica.

Classic Era Developments
Early Classic (250/300-600 CE): Establishment of Long Count calendar in Maya lowlands.
Middle Classic (400-700 CE): Gulf Coast and Teotihuacan influenced/dominated horizon.
Late Classic (600-900 CE): War and urban collapse in Maya region and Mesoamerica.
Early Postclassic: Rise of Toltec, Itza, Putun, and other war-like mercantile empires.

The Postclassic Period
Late Postclassic (1250-1520 CE): Period dominated by Mixtec and Aztec civilizations.
Spanish Colonial (1520-1821 CE): Conquest of Aztec and Spanish exploration, colonization, and settlement.
Early Mexican Republic (1821-1910 CE): Period extending from the fall of the Spanish empire and ending with the onset of the Mexican Revolution.
Mexican Revolution (1910-1920 CE): War that led to the liberal Constitution of 1917.
