Resources and Empire
Tribute system: Mesoamerica: From Gran Chichimeca (Deserts of North) to Soconusco or Coastal Guatemala.
Topographic & Climatic Diversity: Altitude & rainfall.
Tribute Resources: Jaguar pelts, eagles, exotic bird feathers, gold, jade, obsidian, cacao, rubber, cotton, amaranth, corn, textiles, paper, herbs…

Early Humans & Their Art
Mesoamerica: Human settlement 30,000 years.
Early Art: Tequixquiac llama pelvis carved into “coyote” – circa 10,000 years.
Petroglyphs & Pictographs: Presence throughout Mesoamerica for most all time periods.
Early ceramics: 2200 BCE. Pacific coastal Guatemala (S. American influence) & Pre-Olmec Mexican Gulf Coast.
Olmec Civilization (1500-300 BCE): Ceramics, sculpture (Monuments & figurines), mosaics, architecture, jade lapidary, exotic mirrors, etc. (e.g. El Baul, Guatemala)
