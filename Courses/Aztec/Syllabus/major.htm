Major Deliverables

Major Deliverables are those major project reports, midterm and end term reviews, and class presentations completed through the course of the semester. Major Deliverables in this context include Reading Reviews and related written works. There will be a total of ten or more potential Lab Deliverables requiring postings to the Discussion Forum and six or so Major Deliverables for the semester, and they are as follows (topics and deadlines are subject to change):

Unlike Lab Deliverables, Major Deliverables should be submitted on or before the due date in hardcopy to your professor. Materials transmitted via email should be sent to archaeology_institute@csumb.edu and should follow the following format example:

Example: Both the name of the email and the attached document for Major Deliverable 1 (MD1) for the Fall 2005 offering of SBS 348 submitted by John Doe should be titled in the following manner: sbs348F05MD1Doe. Where sbs348 designates the class number, F05 the Fall 2005 term, MD1 Major Deliverable 1, and Doe the last name of the student submitting the deliverable in question. [Note: Your professor reserves the right to reject any or all emailed Deliverables submissions that do not adhere to the email Subject Line and document file name format so noted].

Major Deliverables Guidelines

MD 1: Aztec Site Reports (Oral Presentation, 5-10 minutes)
MD 2: Midterm Self Assessment (See MT Questionnaire)
MD 3: Midterm Book Review of Pre-Approved Text (3-5 Pages, See Reader Response Guidelines)
MD 4: Ten Reader Response Essays (Selected Chapters, See Reader Response Guidelines)
MD 5: Final Art Project or Report and Oral Presentation (See Term Projects and Presentations)*
MD 6: End term Self Assessment (See ET Questionnaire)
MD 7: Final Project Portfolio (See Guidelines or Sample)

*Note: The final project may consist of a standard written report, or other crafted art production replete with detailed project development notes and library background research. Projects that do not exhibit an appropriate level of preliminary (scholarly) research and study will not be accepted for final project credit.
