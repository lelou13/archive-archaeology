Primary Readings

Primary readings or texts chosen for this course have been selected for the purposes of serving as guides to in-class lab exercises and discussion forum activities. The Townsend (2000), and Carrasco and Matos Moctezuma (2003), books constitute the core readings. We will in effect begin the term with the Townsend (2000) text as it provides a comprehensive culture history of the Aztec empire. The Carrasco and Matos Moctezuma (2003) text in turn provides a point of departure for exploring the ideology, religion, and beliefs of the Mexica Aztec. The Knab (2004) text serves as an essential counterpart to the other two texts in that it relates contemporary manifestations of Nahua thought and culture, curing, and cosmology. The Coe and Koontz (2000) text provides an essential narrative concerned with Mexico outside the Maya region. Your midterm Reader Response will focus on this text.
