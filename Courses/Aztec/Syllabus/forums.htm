Forums

Most all lab and field classes offered by the CSU Monterey Bay Institute for Archaeology incorporate the use of Online Journals and participation in Discussion Forums and the like. The purpose for using the electronic or Internet-based journal or forum format is so as to provide an ongoing basis for documenting the class, and lab and field, activities and experiences of all students participating in the Institute’s programs and learning experiences.

 


A Diego Rivera mural from the Palacio Nacional depicts the brutality of the Spanish, Mexico City. Photo (c) Ruben G. Mendoza, 2005.

Whether engaged in an in class lab exercise, video review, or archaeological field experience, your total learning experience will be documented by way of the aforementioned forums. In this way your respective Professor may document and assess your overall participation and ability to produce notes that reflect your individual lab and field experiences, not to mention your group and team-based learning experiences.
While in the past we have permitted open access to your Online Journals, the Discussion Forum format was instituted in the Spring term of 2003 and is set in a pHp-based MySQL server database format. In this way we may more effectively secure your commentaries and journal entries from broader public scrutiny. Please do feel free to ask questions, make comments (both pro and con) regarding those issues discussed in class, or simply post your daily and weekly observations through the course of the semester in the Discussion Forum venue. While your Professor may not be able to respond to all postings to the Discussion Forum, he or she will endeavor to post commentaries that address collective concerns or questions and issues of relevance to the field of archaeology and anthropology more generally.

See http://archaeology.csumb.edu/phpbb2 for the Online Journals archive and links to commentaries posted by students in the past. In addition, the links posted at the top provide shortcuts to the new Discussion Forum used for current course and lab offerings.
