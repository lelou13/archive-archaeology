Welcome

Welcome to the SBS 225 / 325 (Art of the Aztec Empire) online syllabus and Internet site. This course offering was designed by your professor to serve a unique active learning experience. By active learning we are referring to the addition of in-class lab, and or field project-based learning experiences, in which you, the student, are offered the opportunity to engage in an authentic research experience in a hands-on or applied lab or field setting.

In a word, it is the intent of this applied learning experience to put you in the shoes of the Mesoamerican archaeologist or Mexika scholar…all the while making learning come to life in each week’s lab offerings.

Unlike most area studies, regional archaeology, or art history courses, this learning experience will (a) offer weekly opportunities to analyze, question, and interpret archaeological and epigraphic data specific to Aztec and Mesoamerican art and culture, (b) provide basic insights into the archaeological and ethnohistorical analysis and study of Aztec monuments, ceramics, stone tools, architecture, and material culture, (c) permit you to demonstrate your learning via written narratives and online journals as opposed to exams or tests, (d) empower you to self-select those lab projects and learning experiences that you wish to carry through to a final project report, and (f) provide you the opportunity to work with authentic archaeological specimens where available. In effect, this learning experience will train you to see the world through the medium of ancient material culture and its cultural contexts.

In order to make possible the aforementioned in-class labs, you should be prepared to undertake group activities, discussion forums, and analytical tasks -- such as Aztec glyph interpretation -- on Thursday of each and every week through the course of the term. On in-class lab days you will participate in the conduct of selected research and applied learning projects. Projects will vary from semester to semester, but range from the hands-on study of the Aztec writing system, calendrics, astronomy, and the pantheon of ancient Mesoamerican deities. Other project labs will seek to have you work in project teams so as to interpret relevant data and art history, and study some of the most pressing problems in Aztec or Nahuatl studies.
