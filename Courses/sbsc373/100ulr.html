SBSC 100A

Introduction to the Social and Behavioral Sciences

University Learning Requirements (ULR's)

SBSC 100 Partially fulfills the following ULR'S. For a complete list of the ULR'S See pages 32-33 of CSUMB 96-97 Catalogue)

  1. Demonstration of effective oral and written communications skills English Communication (ENG COM)
  2. Demonstration of value for and ability to appreciate origins of another culture and to function and be empathetic in an another culture; ULR: Culture
  3. Demonstration of the ability to use technological tools effectively; includes using electronic communication in class participation and assignments. ULR: Technology
  4. Demonstration of ability to acquire, interpret, and use appropriate information from diverse sources. ULR: Information (INFO)
  5. Demonstration of an understanding of ethical considerations in the building of equity and the manifestation and dynamics of power differentials. ULR: EQUITY
  6. Demonstration of skills in the analysis of political, economic, historical, cross-cultural dimensions of significant historical issues and process taking national and international perspectives; includes perspectives of under represented communities and their histories. ULR HIST (History).
  7. Demonstration of skills in the application of the scientific method in the analysis of complex problems in the social sciences; includes consideration of political, economic, and ethical issues in that analysis. ULR: SCI (Science)
  8. Demonstration of knowledge and skills development in study and application of sociocultural analysis. ULR: CP (Community Participation)
