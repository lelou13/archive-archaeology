Lab Activities

Where pertinent, SBS 332 option labs will draw upon resources related to between three and five hands-on activities that complement course content. The labs in question entail both readings and hands-on or applied activities in which you will engage the basics of a specific analytical or interpretive exercise. Examples of the types of learning modules that we will undertake this semester are described on this portion of the website, and any attendant Internet materials required for the labs will be posted on this page during the course of the semester. Depending on class interest and in-class activities, project labs will vary from semester to semester.

Lab Module 1: Artifact and Specimens Analysis

This lab will engage student participants in the hands-on study and interpretation of historic and pre-historic archaeological materials. Specimens and artifacts subjected to analysis and interpretation will be drawn from existing Institute for Archaeology specimens collections recovered from sites currently under study and excavation. From time to time the professor will introduce materials for discussion from his own personal collections.

Lab Module 2: Faunal Analysis and Bone Identification

Drawing on authentic samples of the many faunal or animal bone specimens recovered from the archaeological deposits of Old Mission San Juan Bautista and other area sites, students will be introduced to the means by which bones are recovered, analyzed, and interpreted for cultural meaning.

Lab Module 3: Mission Ceramics and Virtual Collections

Participants in this lab will be introduced to the study and interpretation of ceramics and pottery as this pertains to the California missions. This lab will require the use of a software program titled “Mission Ceramics: A Virtual Type Collection.” Using the program in question, as well as authentic ceramic specimens recovered from archaeological sites currently under study by your professor, you will be given the opportunity to make use of a virtual type collection for the analysis and identification of historic and pre-historic ceramics. See http://archaeology.csumb.edu/Multimedia/MissionCeramicsProject/index.htm for screen shots of the software program used in this lab project.

Lab Module 4: The Mystery Fossil

This lab entails the use of a software package in which you will use virtual reconstructions of early hominid fossils in an effort to get at the comparative evolutionary anatomy of our earliest primate ancestors. This lab also makes use of fossil casts, skeletal materials, and modern human skeletal reproductions for the purposes of getting at morphological and functional variations in modern human anatomy.

Lab Module 5: Ancient Games and Gamesmanship

Participants will be challenged to learn one of two ancient games -- mainly, African Mancala or Aztec Patolli. In each instance, you will first learn the basic rules of your selected game via online or electronic versions noted at those Internet links posted. Each student participant will then be challenged to compete for as much as ten percentage points of extra credit. Your goal is to both learn the game as well as a bit of the psychology of the culture that produced the game in question.
