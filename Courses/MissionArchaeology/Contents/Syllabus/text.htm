Primary Readings

Primary readings or texts selected for this course have been selected for the purposes of serving as guides to in-class lab exercises and discussion forum activities. Articles from the Fagan (2001) volume have been penned by leading scholars, but nevertheless address some of the most intriguing and popular questions of the day. The Feder (2002) book will in turn provide a point of departure for exploring popular myths and preconceptions about world prehistory and the ancient peoples who populated the landscapes of said world:

Required Texts

Ryan, Donald P. 1999. The Complete Idiot's Guide To Lost Civilizations. Indianapolis, Indiana: Alpha Books.

Fagan, Brian M. 2001. The Seventy Great Mysteries of the Ancient World: Unlocking the Secrets of Past Civilizations. New York: Thames & Hudson, Inc.

Feder, Kenneth L. 2002. Frauds, Myths, and Mysteries: Science and Pseudoscience in Archaeology. Fourth Edition. Boston: McGraw-Hill/Mayfield.
Note: Additional readings will be used to supplement the primary texts, and will include both Reserve Desk readings, Internet reports, and related sources. In addition, you should begin to review those resources noted under Online Journals (at this website) for the purposes of determining which of those world civilizations covered in class will serve as the basis for your own term project.
