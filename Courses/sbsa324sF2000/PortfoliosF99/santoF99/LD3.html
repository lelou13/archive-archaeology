Material Culture 3
9/10/99
The first artifacts I received were very different. The first one is brownish red in color and roughly 1.5" - 2" in length. Side A is fairly smooth, but it has been worked to form sharp edges. There are very few hard edges, where flaking has occurred on the face, and there is a peak in the center that slopes to the edges. The edges are smooth for the most part. It appears that the tip is missing and the bottom has one large notch opposite the point. Side B is smooth except for one small area that appears to be broke off a large rock. This side doesn't appear to have been worked. It resembles the Archaic Humbolt Basal-notch (b), 7,850 - 2,500 B.P. This piece might have been used for a spear point, or as a hand-held cutting tool. The second artifact is gray in color over the majority of it except for a small area on the left side of the base, which is red/pink. It is roughly 1" - 1.25" in length. The flaking appears to be done in the all directions, and it appears to be fairly asymmetrical. The edges are rough, like a serrated knife. There are grooves on the sides near the base for the tie to go around when assembling a spear or arrow. It resembles Late Plains Archaic Kobold Site (a) 3,100 - 1,400 B.P. The two pieces are different in almost all ways.
