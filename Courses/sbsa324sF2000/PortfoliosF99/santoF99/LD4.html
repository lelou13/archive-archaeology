Excavation Methods Review

LD 4 Definitions
9/30/99

Site Grid: Archaeologists stake and line full squares starting from the datum point, and it makes the grid permanent.

Datum: A fixed reference point for the excavation making the mapping procedure easier and also giving people the same point of perspective. Also when someone goes back months later they can find exactly what the others had noted, and therefore no confusion is made between different archaeologists.

Sampling Strategy: Archaeologists sample and excavate various small units within a larger unit to determine whether there is anything of importance in the area. They can then determine whether it is worth the time, the money, and the effort to excavate any further or to move onto another site.

Soil Analysis: Archaeologists take a soil sample and analyze the composition of it to determine of there was human habitation there.

Transect or Pedestrian Surveys: Walking straight lines to find surface evidence of human habitation.
