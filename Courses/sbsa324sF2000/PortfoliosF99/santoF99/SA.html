End Term Self Assessment

    This semester has allowed me to look at the field of Archeology from within.  Since I was a little girl when I visited many Native American ruins I was interested in the field of Archaeology and everything that goes into it.  Through this class I was able to see just the beginning of what archaeology really entails.  I was intrigued by the different labs and the lectures about all the different places Professor Mendoza had been.  I really enjoyed the lecture about the Anastazi and the ruins located in the Southwest.  I visited them when I was about 10 years old and I will never forget what I saw.  Most people have no idea what I am talking about when I mention the Anastazi, so I was glad to see someone else who had been there and knew what I was talking about.  I was also given the opportunity to try a new type of study called Garbology.  It is a relatively new method of studying a culture and habits through rubbish and refuse.  I am also glad to have this opportunity to reflect on the semester as a whole and be able to look back at what I have learned and how it has affected me.

    As I stated in my Midterm Self-Assessment I am extremely excited to be able to take this class.  It allows me to explore a section of Social Science that has intrigued me for many years.  I know that I am lucky that this university offers this class.  Many of my friends who attend other universities envy me and I am glad that I took advantage of that opportunity.  When I first came to this school, three years ago, I saw this course listed on an ULR sheet in my Proseminar class and I knew that I would take it before I left this university.  It was an added bonus that this course fulfilled part of my science ULR.

    This semester I have looked at Material Cultures and the study of past civilizations through the remains of their possessions and trash; the study of stone tool making; and Garbology.  Material Cultures and Garbology sections of the class were the ones that I was most interested in.  I like the Material Cultures section because it showed how archaeologists look at the remains left my people and determine the habits and aspects of their lives.  The study of the Anastazi is similar in that archaeologists are studying the ruins in the hope of finding out what happened to the people who suddenly disappeared.  I liked the videos and lectures that explained many different processes that archaeologists use in the study of past civilizations.

    As far as Garbology is concerned I was curious as to what you can learn from people’s garbage.  I was curious as to how much about a person you can learn from their garbage, and that if your assumptions would prove to be correct or not.  My partner and I decided early in the semester that our final project would be on Garbology.  We chose to study our peers.  We chose two dorm room, one with two girls and the other with two boys.  We wanted to look at whether or not our stereotypes of a male and female college student were correct.  We made assumptions as to what we would find in the beginning and the plan is to see if any of them were correct.  We made the hypothesis that the boys would eat more fast food and drink more alcohol that the girls; and that the girls would eat more healthy food than the boys would.  For the most part we were correct, there were many wrappers from fast food in the boys garbage, and there were lots of fruit remnants and yogurt in the girls trash.  We were not totally surprised by this finding, but we didn’t expect almost everything to be correct.

 I am really glad that I took this course.  I am glad that I have had a chance to take a peek into the field of Archaeology.  I was able to see that it is more like a hobby than a future career.  I liked the different labs and everything, but I came to learn that deep down I am a film person.  I would however like to incorporate Archaeology into my filmmaking, by perhaps, doing documentaries on the Anastazi and other lost civilizations.  No one know what the future holds for me, but I will definitely keep what I have learned in this course in my mind for future film projects.
