End Term Oral Report
 

 There were such lines as "Archeologists, my life is in ruins" and "Archeologists tell forgotten stories," with these lines we

can pretty much tell what it is that the life of an archaeologist consists of.  When I was doing my project at the

Mission of San Juan Bautista, I found that Professor Ruben Mendoza would park his truck near the places where we

were performing our excavation.  He would do this do to the loading and unloading of the equipment that we were

to use for the excavation every Friday.  While I saw his truck I found that he had some bumper stickers.  One of them

read "Archaeologists tell forgotten stories" I found that this is true.  Every time that I would find something that would

look interesting to me I would call Professor Mendoza, so that I could show him what is was that I had found and also

so that he can tell me what the artifact meant.  Professor Mendoza would come over and say, "This is interesting!

You know I believe this was used to perform…" and he would go on to tell me a story of how the artifact was used.

By saying all this I simply prove that his bumper sticker says the truth and especially about him.

     While at the mission I found that at certain levels, in my square, there was a lot of debris. Debris is a useful study aid

as it is how archaeologists make generalizations and assumptions in the field.  By examining debris, one can deduce

where and what stone tools where made, and in what fashion.  Melissa and I would find lots of debris and most of it

just showed that it had been burnt.  By this we were able to tell that we were digging into a possible fire pit.  At the

beginning there was a lot of evidence that this would be the case, but it was not until I got to the end of the

second level when I was able to tell that we were in fact digging inside a fire pit.  I feel that my overall experience at

the Mission in San Juan Bautista was great and of high learning.  Sometimes I would stop and think if there was

anything else to archaeology than just getting dirty, digging a lot, sifting, and logging.  I found that there is a lot

more to this than just what I mentioned.  Professor Mendoza did a good job in showing us what the different choices

are when becoming an archaeologist.  In class we saw all of the videos in which Professor Mendoza worked and all of

the recognition that he received from different news channels.  Overall I feel that this class was one of the most

interesting ones from which I learned a lot and gained extracurricular knowledge.  Thanks Professor Mendoza.
