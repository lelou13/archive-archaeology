Project Report: Excavation at The San Juan Bautista Mission

In having participated in a total of six excavations at the San Juan Bautista Mission, my experiences range so differently from excavation day 1 to excavation day 6. Although all six days may seem quite similar, something new always happened or some interesting artifact somehow managed to show up amongst my day’s findings. The following journal entries all illustrate and clearly draw each days’ experience and findings. The journal entries best define the learning outcomes I attribute to the excavations and the holistic purpose of the project. The purpose of the excavation is to locate the old foundation that once existed along with the rest of the currently standing parts of the Mission. Although the diggings became tedious at time and painful due to annoying blisters, the over-all purpose as a student working at the mission was still clear. As a student at the mission participating with the excavations, the learning outcome goals intended were to apply classroom lectures, readings and videos to real hands-on fieldwork. The excavations at the San Juan Bautista Mission allowed me to have this experience and is best illustrated through my excavation journal entries.

Theresa F. Mendoza
