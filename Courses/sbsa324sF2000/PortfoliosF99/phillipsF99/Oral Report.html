Oral Report Outline
SBSC 224
Archaeology: From Map to Museum
Fall 1999

Introduction
The topic I chose for my semester project was the Lithics Lab Experiment. The theme of this experiment was the replication of stone tools from obsidian or jasper resulting in the creation of an excavation site for observation and analysis.
Procedures
· Setting up of the lab
· Gridded tarp
· Tools and cores
· Gear for safety
· Flintknapping process
· Collection of debitage
· Recording of size and weight of pieces
· Analysis
· Cutting tests
· Observation under microscope
Conclusion
This project successfully introduced the field of archaeology, exploring many facets of the vocation, including a virtual prehistoric site and stone tool production. In using the scientific method to carry on an experiment from start to finish, I could see how this situation could be analogous to other fields and daily life situations as well.
