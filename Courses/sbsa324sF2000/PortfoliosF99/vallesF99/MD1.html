Major Deliverable 1:  Self Assessment




1. I have two main purposes for enrolling in Archaeology:  From Map to Museum.  One of them is the fact that I recently found that I may have had ancestors who inhabited this region of California, and it is of my main interest to learn as much about it as I possibly can.  The other reason which I came to find out after being enrolled is that this course covers the science part of the University learning requirement that I've yet to complete.  My objectives are to further my research of the peoples both indigenous and immigrant missionaries who made this central region of California their home--so that I can trace my family tree.  I have already uncovered which indigenous peoples lived here and on the mission of San Juan Bautista's behalf, I now carry a basic understanding of the geographical whereabouts the tribes in the central region made their homes.

2. I have participated in the lab and field deliverable to an unsatisfactory degree, I do not feel I have contributed fully through my participation.  I have missed 3 class meetings and have missed 3 San Juan Bautista archeaological meetings.  I have tried my hardest, but do not quite recall if I have or have not met the deliverable's schedule.  I have committed myself to an extent that has room for improvement, but am confident that I will very much so improve in the class meetings and deliverables to come.

3. I have used the online journal with no problems, however, I don't feel that the quality of my journals is that which meets the requirements.  I feel this way because I don't have sufficient time in class to reflect and mentally process what we have gone over in the class meetings.  I feel if I had more time to reflect and digest the information gained in the meetings, I would have more developed and elaborated journals.

4. I have progressed immensely, before this course I had only one perspective from which I understood many of the things we have gone over, particularly when it comes to Aztec and Mayan cultural and traditional beliefs.   I had only the "Calpulli", and "Curandero" perspective and now ,  I have an unbiased academic point of view.  As I have mentioned prior to this point, I have gained a physical sense of where my ancestors may have possibly been from.  There are still many questions unanswered, but I have learned more about the indigenous practices and have unlearned what I thought I had already unleared before I received this unbiased scholarly perspective.  I appreciate and understand the archaeological concepts because I have gone to the excavation site in San Juan and as a result to being a visual learner, this hands-on training had helped me grasp the different concepts involved in archaeological research.  I will be doing both the archaeological site and a final project report because I have not been able to attend all the excavation meetings-due to lack of transportation, and in hopes to uncover more of my ancestral history.

5. I intend to do a research paper comparing and contrasting the academic to the first hand Aztec cultural traditions and I plan to use direct interviews with "Jefas/Jefes," "Calpullis," and online library journals, books, films based on ethnographies and perhaps (if you are available) an interview with you.
6. I am fascinated with all concepts and areas of study we have covered to this point in the semester.  Pretty much all concepts and ideas we have covered will be reflected in my overview-type research paper.

7. I enjoyed everything because I feel I am learning and taking in different aspects and concepts from cultures I feel I am a product of.  The only project I could have done with out, but I still feel it's necessary has been the extensive reading on flintknapping.  I am a visual learner and it has not helped to read about it.  You may want to keep it only because it serves as a handbook/manual sort of reading for the actual flintknapping project.  I would recommend handing it out before the actual project as opposed to after, so that students can have it with them while the do the actual flintknapping.

8. I plan on improving my overall performance in this course by not missing any class meetings and trying to make it to the rest of the San Juan excavation meetings.  I also need to better my journals and keep up with the deadlines.  My primary strengths are coming in with an open mind eager to learn.  What I have been learning as far as the Aztec and Mayan codices is very contradictory to what I thought I had learned before the course.  I still take in both sides only now I come up with my own conclusions.
