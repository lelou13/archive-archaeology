Author: Brenda Valles.- Subject: Excavations.- 6 of 14Records Found.

Date: 9/14/99.- Time: 11:29:06 PM.- Entry Num: 195
On Friday 9/10/99 we went to Old San Juan where we where oriented to the excavation process. We were given the opportunity to be paired up with someone, I paired myself up with a community member named Maria. We were dealing with an area at the 20 cm level. We worked right next to the wall of the west convento wing and were able to uncover
many interesting things. We found the common tile, and we also came across what appears to be bone. We came across what appears to be pellets or beebees, that was interesting because nothing of that nature had been found. We washed the material that we found and the next step was to label and log material found. It was a learning experience and I can't wait to go again, because now I know to a certain extent what to expect. I think this hands on activity gives us the opportunity to better understand what we read and discuss in class.

9/17/99 On this day we excavated at the same level, which was between 20 and 40 centimeters. I came across bone, several small pieces of different colored glass, wood, charcoal, seeds, and what appears to be beebees or some sort of metal round ball, ceramic, a piece of Spanish brick, and tile. It was a good turn-out, and I feel it is the best way to understand the vocabulary and concepts discussed in class and in the readings.
