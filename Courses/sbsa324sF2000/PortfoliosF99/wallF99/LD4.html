Excavation Methods Review

Stratigraphy: The soil levels and strata distinguished by differences in color and texture, at a site, and how these are associated with each other and the cultural aspects of the site.

Soil Analysis: Some of the soil from an excavation is collected for further study in the laboratory. Among the types of analysis would be for flora and fauna information.

Screening: A method used where soil is sifted through a screen in order to single out smaller items that might be overlooked in the excavation process such as beads, bone fragments, etc.

Features:  items such as fire hearths, artifact concentrations, sleeping and eating areas that can be found at excavation sites.

Test pits: Metric square areas used to determine whether or not an area is a good area to excavate. Three- dimensional control must also be maintained in test pits. Exploratory excavations.

Datum Point: A fixed point where all other site measurements are taken from.

Research Design: A systematic transect sampling strategy. A method of random sampling.

Excavation Tools: In the past, pick and shovel were used, then backhoes, and now excavation tools include remote sensing such as aerial photography, soil resistivity, ground penetrating radar, and proton magnetometry.

Instrument Maps: maps made by using technologies such as soil resistivity surveys. Water content is measured and high density areas such a foundations, paths, and roads tend to hold more water.
