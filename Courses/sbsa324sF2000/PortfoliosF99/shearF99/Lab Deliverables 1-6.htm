Author: Ashley Shear.- Subject: LD: 5

Date: 9/30/99.- Time: 11:30:59 AM.- Entry Num: 278

1. The tools that were used in the search for the "Lost Spanish Mission" according to David Hurst Thomas was Random Sampling transect sampling strategy, gasoline powered posthole digger, and remote sensing. When using random sampling the archaeology team walks sections of the land. Walking in a series of east west transects each a 100 meters wide by using this method you cover a lot of ground and can maybe find a site. This isn't always the best way because you might end up never finding a site. There might not be anything on the surface that is why they use many methods. Using transect sampling you take a 20% randomized sample. You investigate each site digging test units to find any hint of artifacts. When using the Gasoline-powered posthole digger it throws up a sampling of dirt that can be used to find artifacts. This method is very helpful because it allows the archaeologist to sample a deep hole in a matter of minutes. This allows them to see if it is in fact a site. 2. Archaeologist dig strait holes and make strait lines because that way they can see the midden and can actually tell when someone was there and what type of things they eat and what they did. It is also easier to tell exactly where an artifact was found whereas if you dug a hole there would be less accuracy. When digging square holes it enables the archaeologist to give exact directions to a site. You have to take it slow or else you might miss something of importance or might break something. Drawing from my own experience out at Mission San Juan Buatista. By digging holes that are square it makes it easier to chart where we found an artifact. When cataloging the artifacts we found during the day we use N-10 W-38 NE ¼ this tells exactly where we were digging and then you can find the place that the artifact came from.
