With this exercise I learned that excavating a site is not as easy as I had previously thought. It is more than just digging and finding artifacts. Before you even start digging you need to submit a proposal. In this proposal you need to consider number of people involved, the wages of people involved, insurance, number of tools you are going to need, sleeping arrangments, transportation, food, length of time in the field, number of units in the site. The exercise shows us that every little detail must be taken into consideration, the littlest details might hurt your chances of recieving that proposal. However it is easier to resubmit a proposal in a simulater than it is in real life.

Author: Anthony Marrone.- Subject: Importance of maps in Archaeology.- 10 of 10Records Found.
Date: 8/27/99.- Time: 12:23:46 AM.- Entry Num: 64
Maps are an important part to many different areas in archaeology. They allow archaeologists to rediscover as well as discover different historical sites. They aid in the serveing procedure of dense land areas. If the map is layed correctly than a site of interest will never be lost, even if the trails that lead to that site have been covered over. It is not only that map that helps but also the compus as well. The compus allows archaeologists to determine magnetic north and get a sence of were they are in relationship to their surrondings. The compus is another way to ensure that a site will not be lost.
