Archaeology Web Sites

1. http://www.ca-missions.org/

Title: CMSA California Missions scholars
This site gives a 360 degree view of the San Javier Mission. The California Mission Studies Association is a nonprofit group of historians, archaeologists, architects, preservationists, Native Americans and clerics devoted to the study and survival of the 21 Ca. Missions.

2. http://www.standingstones.com/sanjuan.html

3. http://www.student.monterey.edu/dh/evansjustinryan/world/main.html

4. http://theboftime.com/Issue-2/sanxavier.html

Title: The Web of Time Issue Two
· The Web of Time features current and interesting preservationist projects. The site features the preservation being at the San Xavier mission in Arizona.

5. http://www.artifacts.org/linkpage.html

Title: Linkpage
· This site provides its viewers with an annotated listing of selected Spanish history and heritage sites. Emphasis has been placed on sites relating to Spain’s colonial activities on North America…other http://www.artifacts.org/linkpage.htm

6. http://www.upf.com/archaeol.html

Title: University of Florida’s –Archaeology
· This site gives pre-Columbian native American peoples archaeology and historical information on  Southwestern native Americans, also.

7. http://southflorida.digitalcit…family/features/

Title: Florida’s Indian Post
· This site gives information to explore nearby areas of Florida’s missions and their current archaeological excavations.

8. http://www.elp.rr.com/mission

Title: Visit the Missions: Missions Ysleta
· This site gives information and a good overview of Mission Ysleta in California.

9. http://www.studionet.it/flint1.html

Title: The Lost Heritage Flintknapping School
· This site gives explains the history behind flintknapping. Excellent site.

10. http://w3.netlinkcorp.com

Title: The art of Flintknapping
· This site gives a step by step guide to make stone tools.
