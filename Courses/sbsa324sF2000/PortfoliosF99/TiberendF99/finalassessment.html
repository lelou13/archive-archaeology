Final Self Assessment

 I have fulfilled my objectives in SBSA 224/324S that will satisfy the HCOM upper division concentration requirement.  I decided to enroll in Dr. Mendoza’s class because HCOM doesn’t have many history classes that I am interested in taking.  Dr. Mendoza’s archaeology class covers a period in history that I am interested in.  We covered historical information about Mezzo-America and other early Native American cultures.  After completing this class, I have a better understanding about the early Aztec and Mayan culture in Mexico and how these cultures left a lasting imprint upon the world of history. I intend to use the material I have learned in this class within my senior capstone.  I’m not sure yet what my capstone will encompass, but I hope to research Native-American culture in the Southwest.

 In addition, this class introduced the methods, principals, and practices of field archaeology by using exercises that stress strategy, interpretation, description, information management, archaeological technologies, and the role of scientific inquiry in the human sciences. I completed Major Deliverables in class and in the labs on Thursdays.  I also completed all of the Lithics (Stone tools) labs. The Lithics lab introduced the hands-on manufacture and use of stone tools and the ancient and modern material cultures.  Within these labs, I learned what it must have been like to work and make different forms of raw material (mainly obsidian) into stone tools.  The work was fun and gave me a challenge.  Towards the end of the Lithics Lab, the class was instructed to make three types of stone tools.  At first, I thought that I would not be able to turn my piece of obsidian into anything.  However, with determination, I was able to produce a cutting tool, scrapper, and an arrowhead.
 I have enjoyed many of the Lab and Field Deliverables.  Given that I am suppose to give a final assessment whether or not I have meet my objectives; I would say that I have improved and completed all of the assignments.  One of my objectives was to discuss with Dr. Mendoza why I couldn’t arrive to class until about 8:15.  I had mentioned in mid term self assessment that I thought more explanation about the in class lab material was needed.  However, Dr. Mendoza covered this material when the class first started.  I did discuss the problem I was having getting to class at 8:00.  I am a single parent which leaves I am the one who has to commute my daughter to school each day.  My daughter starts school around 8:00 so I wasn’t able to arrive then.  Also, I thought I had discussed this problem with Dr. Mendoza in the beginning of the semester.  This was an over site on my part.

 In addition, I started to ask more questions in class; speaking up in class has always been difficult for me.  I find that I able to speak up more when I am in small groups or with people that I feel comfortable with.
 Keeping up with the class readings was another goal, I did keep up with the readings and I finished all of the assigned readings within the textbook and the class handouts.  The reading and the handouts helped to clear up any questions and the reading material helped to reinforce the archaeological material that was covered in class.
 The most important objective I have completed is working on the archaeology dig at the San Juan Bautista Mission.  I wanted to learn first hand how to do field excavations and analysis of Hispanic colonial artifacts at the Old Mission.  The field excavations also fulfill the service learning requirement in HCOM.  Although I have already meet this requirement, I wanted to learn more about the San Juan Bautista Mission.  I have visited almost all of the California Missions because I wanted to learn more about California early history.
 Overall, I would say that my objectives for taking this class have been met and I now have a better understanding about the role archaeology plays in understanding and preserving history for future generations.  I have enjoyed Dr. Mendoza’s class and I would not suggest that anything should be changed within the class content.

 Finally, I did not know what I was doing at the excavation site, and now I have learned what is important and how work on excavation sites.  Furthermore, I would conclude the most important concept I have learned about archaeology is that “the present can be seen to serve the past”, it is important to understand and preserve these ancient cultures for future generations before they are lost forever….
