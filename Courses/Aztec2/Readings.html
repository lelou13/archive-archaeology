About the Preliminary Bibliography

You should consider the following bibliography as a preliminary resource list for any study of Aztec art and culture.  While the "Aztec Art Readings" portion of the list is very preliminary, particularly as much has been accomplished by art historians and architects in Mesoamerica, the "Aztec Era Documents" section consists of those resources from which many later studies have developed ideas about Aztec art and culture.  While parimary resources are not always readily available, they are to be considered a preferred resources for primary research and study into Aztec society.  All else is essentially secondary research...but no less valuable for the insights it has to offer.  Please note that Dr. Michael Smith was kind enough to provide a number of those readings listed in the "Aztec Art Readings" section of this preliminary bibliography.  I have attached his note regarding those contributions he provided.

A Note From Dr. Michael E. Smith

"Umberger is the only real synthesis of Aztec- and Aztec-related art throughout the empire, although Elizabeth Boone's book Stories in Red and Black (and her chapter in Aztec Imperial Strategies) has good information on codices. What hasn't been done are studies of local objects and styles and how they relate (or not) to Aztec objects and styles, looking at ethnicity, political patterns, exchange, etc.  You could have students look at specific provincial areas and compare the ceramics or architecture or sculptures to Aztec patterns and try to account for what they find."

Michael E. Smith, Professor
Department of Anthropology, SS-263
University at Albany, SUNY
1400 Washington Ave
Albany, NY 12222
