Ancient Mesoamerica: Cultural and Historical Foundations
Professor Ruben G. Mendoza
CSU Monterey Bay

Pre-Aztec History: Peoples
Olmec (People of the Land of Rubber): ca. 1500-300 BCE; La Venta, Tres Zapotes, San Lorenzo.
Olmecoid (Mesoamerican peoples influenced by Olmec mercantile and colonial expansion)
Settlements: Tlatilco, Ticoman, Copilco, Tlapacoya, and Cuicuilco: Pre-Olmec and Olmecoid.
Highland Olmec: Cuicuilco, Mexico, and Chalcatzingo, Morelos. Monumental architecture, ceramics, sculpture, jade and obsidian…etc.

Highland Olmec Presence
Gulf Coast/Highland interactions: Tlatilco, etc.
Highland/Coastal interactions:  (Juxtlahuaca, Guerrero, and Teopantecuanitlan, Morelos)
Cuicuilco: Olmecoid mercantile center.
Chalcatzingo: Rock carvings & settlement.
La Pilas, Morelos: Olmec to Teotihuacan transition.

Proto-Classic Developments
Cuicuilco’s Demise: Eruption of Xitle and the Rise of Teotihuacan, ca. 200 BCE.
Teotihuacan (Place Where Men Become Gods): 100 BCE=settlement; 100 CE= Construction of Sun Pyramid; 250 CE = Temple of Quetzalcoatl & Cuidadela; Avenue of the Dead.
Epiclassic Collapse: 550-650 CE=Competing mercantile interests; Teotihuacan Corridor closes; 650 CE= City Burned; 750 CE=City abandoned; 850 CE= Toltecs occupy ruined city.
Olmecoid Influence: Gulf Coast presence in city.

Teotihuacán’s Legacy
Urban Center: 22 sq. km; ca. 250,000 pop.; urban planning; water works…
Cultural Legacy: Origins of Mexican civilization… Aztec legends, birth of Sun; Moctezuma’s pilgrimages…
Miguel Covarrubias’ Art History (from Olmec to Aztec…Were-Jaguar/Tlaloc)
Arts: Architecture, painting, sculpture, writing, lapidary...

Epiclassic & Early Postclassic
Historic Olmec or Olmeca-Xicalanca (Dominate highlands from ca. 600-900 CE): Centered at Cacaxtla, Tlaxcala, and Cholula, Puebla.
Toltec and Itza Maya or Putún (Dominate Highlands and Maya Lowlands, ca. 800-1250 CE): Principal centers at Tula, Hidalgo; Cholula, Puebla; Chichen Itza, Yucatan; Xicalango, Tabasco.  Fine Orange & Plumbate ceramics.
Tollan or Tula Hidalgo: Type-site of the Toltecs of Aztec legend; Ce Acatl Topiltzin Quetzalcoatl.
