Egypt: The Nile River Focus
Independent process of civilizational development: i.e.: Primary State Formation.
H-G = agriculture = state = empire…
Highly centralized political system.
Least urban, great bureaucratic complexity, self-sufficient villages/towns, political cycles tied to Nile floods.
Desert: Isolated Egypt from external influence in early period.

Egyptian Chronology

Egyptian Unification
Predynastic (4000-3100 BC): Complex patterns emerge; trade, specialization, settlement…
Archaic/Old Kingdom (3100-2160 BC): Upper & Lower Egypt united politically; writing system; architecture; administration; ideology…
Menes (Narmer): U. conquers L. Egypt; theocratic political systems dominate; capital at Memphis; internal dissension @ 2900 BC despite powerful kings; major construction programs pre-2700 BC.
2700 BC: nation-state, multi-tiered economy, hierarchical bureaucracy, astronomy, geometry, accounting, surgery, architecture…

The Mid-3rd Millennium
Age of Pyramids and Palaces: integrated royal bureacracy
Djoser (2nd King of Old Kingdom): Egyptian economy & society organized; Grand Vizier = Imhotep = pyramid-tomb at Saqqara (6 levels, 60 m, wall = 9m x 1.6 km).
4th Dynasty (2613-2494 BC): King Khufu = pyramids of Giza = W. bank.
Great Pyramid of Giza: 2,300,000 blocks, 2.5 tons each = high degree of political centralization;
Wahkare’ Khety III (2070-2040 BC): Merykare builds towns to counteract fragmentation…rival provinces = provincial governors U. Egypt = Nomarchs (6th Dynasty= 2180 BC = reduced power of pharaoh.

Egypt vs Mesopotamia
Egypt: No standing armies (OK)
No significant slavery
Highly administered, simple redistributive, chiefdom-like…
Economic exchange focused on king
No merchants in Old Kingdom
Social & military conscription…service to King
Mesopotamia: partially capitalistic, multi-tiered, highly differentiated economic system; highly militaristic, significant slavery; competing city-states and palace-based economic organization; incessant warfare, militaristic cosmology and worldview…
