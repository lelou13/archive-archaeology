Course Description

Introduces the methods, principles, and practices of field archaeology by using exercises that stress strategy, interpretation, description, information management, archaeological technologies, and the role of scientific inquiry in the human sciences. Introduces the hands-on manufacture and use of stone tools and the interpretation of ancient and modern material cultures. A field option includes on-site excavations and or analysis of Hispanic colonial artifacts from Old Mission of San Juan Bautista and or San Carlos Borromeo del Rio Carmelo (aka: Carmel).

Note: During the Spring 2003 term the Institute for Archaeology received a grant from a congressional appropriation through the US Department of Education so as to conduct a Wireless Technologies in Archaeology demonstration project. This offering of SBS 224s/324s continues to serve as a demonstration project for wireless technologies. As such, students will be introduced to the use of wireless Pocket or Tablet PC’s and related technologies, and will use such information management tools during the course of lab and field outings in order to prepare Online Journal and or Discussion Forum entries. For further information on this unique and significant project, please see http://archaeology.csumb.edu/wireless/
