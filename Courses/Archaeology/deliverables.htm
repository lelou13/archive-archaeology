Deliverables
In the parlance of the Social and Behavioral Sciences and CSU Monterey Bay, “deliverables” are those byproducts of the learning experience that you will deliver upon request and on schedule. Many granting agencies that fund major projects, and much of the Hollywood motion picture production scene more broadly, use the term “deliverables” to denote those individual elements required in order to see through to completion a much larger project initiative.

Lab Deliverables are those aspects of the learning experience that require a hands-on or other direct engagement with data or team-based and collective efforts to solve or resolve problems and seek interpretations of a body of information or data. Such labs will range from the group-based study of copies of ancient Maya and Aztec era codices or screen-fold books to the identification and study of stone tools. These efforts are intended to provide you some context for understanding those readings and materials provided as a basis for class discussions. In this sense, your participation in class discussions is critical to your Professor’s ability to assess your progress and engagement with the overall learning experience.

While you may draft out a written narrative in an Online Journal, Discussion Forum, or typewritten two-page summary referred to as the Lab Deliverable, it is critical that in each instance in which you participate you prepare some form of documentation of your respective participation. I use these weekly submissions as a basis for judging and grading your overall class and lab participation. While you will receive credit toward the final course grade for completing such Discussion Forum or Journal postings, typewritten Lab Deliverable papers or journal entries are judged on a cumulative end-term basis.

Major Deliverables are those major project efforts that result in the development of such narratives as Midterm and Endterm Self Assessments, Project Proposals, Annotated Bibliographies, and Final Term Project Reports. Major Deliverables serve as the benchmarks upon which your overall class performance will be judged and assessed. It is these items in particular that will be graded on a regular basis so as to provide you with some sense of how you are doing as per the overall class assessment. If you fail to submit Major Deliverables, or to do so in a timely fashion, your Professor will ultimately determine whether or not your efforts will be counted toward the final grade.
