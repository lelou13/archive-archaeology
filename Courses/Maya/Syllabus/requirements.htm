Assessment/Grades

It should be noted that the basis for grading and assessment of this section might be subject to slight variation from what is noted below in the event that the class shifts its emphasis for the purposes of study options and those projects undertaken. Any changes will be subject to class discussion and consensus where appropriate.

Participation: Because this course will require your in-class participation in discussion, lab exercises (such as that of the Gamesmanship and the Art of Patolli, Bul, or Pok Ta Pok), and in your use of the Discussion Forum, 25 points of your final grade will be assessed by way of your attendance and participation. Participation will in part be assessed by way of the timely and thorough completion of (a) Online Journal or Discussion Forum entries completed on a weekly basis, and (b) the documentation of in-class Lab Deliverables (see below) via the Discussion Forum (http://archaeology.csumb.edu/phpbb2) and by way of the submission of supporting class materials related to the Lab Deliverables (25 points).

Note: Because most of the Discussion Forum entries will be completed during class time, and will address class participation and lab exercises, it is critical that your participation be reflected in the Discussion Forums. In fact, your professor uses online and wireless journal entries to assess credit for participation. See online journals at: http://archaeology.csumb.edu/phpbb2.

Finally, please note that much of what we cover in weekly labs will require that you to bring to each class both the Coe & Van Stone (2001) and Tedlock (1996) texts as each of these works contain much of the reference material that we will require for said labs.

The Maya Glyphs Workshop: The Maya Glyphs Workshop will require that you maintain detailed notes of any and all observations or translations had during the course of any given workshop day. You should maintain a journal that documents the workshop. You should include in your journal diagrams or illustrations of key glyphs and monumental inscriptions, where appropriate, as one other way of presenting your information. Notes, illustrations, and diagrams from the Maya Glyphs Workshop will be submitted, along with your Final Portfolios, as one other measure of your participation in in-class labs and discussion group projects (see Participation for credit).

Reader Response Papers: Approximately ten typewritten and single-spaced one page reader response papers will be completed by you and submitted in hardcopy with sufficient detail so that the instructor can assess your thorough completion and understanding of the readings assigned. The reader response papers will account for approximately 30 percentage points of your final semester grade. The reader response papers will be graded at 3 points each per minimum one-page essay if they demonstrate your thorough understanding and reflection of the readings. Two points will be assessed if a partial understanding of the readings are reflected, and one percentage point will be assessed if the essay was completed but did not reflect a clear understanding of the readings assigned (30 percentage points possible). The instructor will reserve the right to assess a No Credit for late papers.

Note: You may forego the Midterm Book Review through the submission of an additional five single-spaced one-page reader response papers, for a total of fifteen Reader Response papers for the term. The articles that you review will be selected in consultation with your professor beforehand, or may be of your own choosing from existing class resources; however, for those articles that you select from beyond existing resources, you must obtain approval from your professor prior to preparing reviews of articles not assigned in class.

Midterm Book Review or Project Proposal: A double-spaced, minimum five-page, midterm book review will be required as one other point of departure for your instructor’s assessment of your performance at midterm. In this instance, the primary text reading for midterm consideration will be drawn from a list of at least five books provided by way of a preliminary bibliography prepared by you that will be submitted in advance for approval by your professor. In order to receive credit for the midterm book review, you will need to show that your professor approved the bibliography submitted by you prior to submission of the midterm book review itself (15 points).

Project Proposal: An approved project proposal option is required of all students who intend to see through an art history or other creative work or arts project. If, for instance, you determine to prepare an architectural diorama of an ancient Maya temple as the basis for your final project, the project proposal should propose a rationale for your respective architectural study and provide a working bibliography. You should provide a three to five page single-spaced summary of the goals and methods of your study and your anticipated results or findings. Archaeology majors are strongly encouraged to more fully develop the project proposal and final report as one means of walking through the process of preparing for original research, such as that that might be expected in preparing for the SBS Capstone in archaeology.

Note: You may use the midterm book review as the basis for your End Term Oral Presentation. In other words, if you read a book on Maya blood sacrifice (e.g., Schele and Miller 1986), you may use that topic and what you learned by way of said review as the basis for your final oral presentation.

Midterm and Endterm Self Assessments: At midterm and end term each class participant will submit a self-assessment statement in which you respond to a series of questions about your overall performance to that respective point in the semester. The questionnaire for this exercise may be found at: http://archaeology.csumb.edu/Guidelines/Questionnaires/SelfAssessment.pdf (10 points total).

Final Project and Oral Narrative: Your end term scholarly objective or requirement is to submit a final project paper (of no less than 10 typewritten double-spaced pages not including bibliography and illustrations) with content based on your own research from reliable sources drawn from at least eight separate and distinct books and or articles (see Format Guidelines at Project Guidelines). I strongly encourage projects that derive directly from our in-class workshops, labs, and or related group exercises. The presentation formats for these final projects or presentations may be negotiated with your professor. The Oral Presentation in this instance should provide no more than a 5 to 10-minute overview of your learning experience specific to those concepts examined in the course of your study (see Presentations guidelines). The Final Project and Oral Presentation or equivalent is worth 25 points towards your final grade (25 points).

· Participation (Discussion Forum Entries): 25
· Reader Response Papers (10 @ 3 pts. each): 30
· Midterm Book Review or Project Proposal (Approved Bibliography): 15
· Midterm and Endterm Self Assessments (2 @ 5 pts. each): 10
· Final Project and Oral Presentation: 25
· Final Portfolio with Table of Contents: 10

· Optional & Tentative Museum Tours or Excavations (Total possible): 10

Total Possible Points: 125 pts
