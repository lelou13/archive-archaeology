Old Views on Maya

 1.    Swidden agriculturalists

 2.    Dispersed peasant populations

 3.    Low population densities

 4.    Vacant ceremonial centers

 5.    Small elite core & specialists

 6.    Optimal rainforest environments

 7.    Minimal reliance on trade & specialization (primarily ceremonial consumption)

 8.    Benign priest-kings preoccupied with time and cyclic recording

 9.    Absence of managerial or bureacratic specialists

10.    Power relations based on religious devotion by peasantry

11.    Maya as dutifully peaceful & civilized (contrast militaristic and uncivil highland Mexican "corruptors")

12.    Economic hegemony, taxation, tribute, and warfare considered alien to Maya

New Views on Maya

1.    Intra-regional hierarchical organization

2.    Centralized and bureacratized political structure

3.    Agricultural intensification requiring administrative supervision

4.    Economic specialization

5.    Long-distance trade in both elite and domestic goods

6.    High population densities

7.    Alliance formation and war and conquest

8.    Existence of regional states at Tikal, Yaxchilan, Copan, and Palenque
