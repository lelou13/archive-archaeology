SBSC 100A

Introduction to the Social and Behavioral Sciences

Learning Experience Description and Objectives

An introduction to the basic principles (theory and method or concept and practice) found in the traditional social science areas of anthropology, archaeology, social history, cultural geography, political science, sociology and allied fields of inquiry. The content and emphasis of this learning experience will vary according to thematic and discipline-based perspectives brought to bear by the specific section leaders.

Prerequisite(s): none. It is recommended that Social and Behavioral Sciences majors also concurrently enroll in a section of SBSC 108A to explore a chosen area of interest by virtue of a hands-on learning lab or field experience in the social sciences Objectives
