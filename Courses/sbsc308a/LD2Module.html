Lab Deliverable 1:
The Video Storyboard

Directions: The Video Storyboard is intended to provide an entry level introduction to the development of a "storyboard" or diagram of the narrative flow of your multimedia or video project.  The essential idea behind the storyboard is to identify the different and distinct components of a multimedia project.  Normally, the storyboard is the first item of business to be completed in planning the development of a multimedia CD-ROM, video, or animated story.  In this instance, you will review the video program entitled "This Old Pyramid," and will build a storyboard using index cards to identify the different and distinct learning components of the video narrative.

1.    Using the index cards provided, prepare to identify the primary theme, and subthemes of the video "This Old Pyramid."  As you identify a theme or subtheme, note the theme, subtheme, or learning exercise on the index card provided for that purpose.

2.    In identifying primary themes, please be sure to note said theme or subtheme on an individual index card.  If for instance, you identify the theme as "The Scientific Method," prepare to identify the subthemes that constitute the primary theme and its visual narrative.  In other words, what subthemes and examples are provided by way of the narrative, and how do these fit into the overall narrative.

3.    As you assess the primary components of the visual narrative, be prepared to identify what you believe to be the principal strategies (or learning modules) employed by the videographers to illustrate those points critical to imparting an understanding of the main themes.

4.    Finally, having identified the primary "Themes" and "Subthemes" of the video narrative, be prepared to identify and discuss those subthemes and "Learning Modules" that you would deem critical to restructuring the video in question; or for creating the makings of a multimedia project for the Old Mission of San Juan Bautista.

5.    This latter portion of the Lab Deliverable will require a written narrative that you will E-mail to your professors at the E-mail links provided in the index page of this syllabus.  You may also e-mail your materials to the link labeled: astv@archaeology.ml.org
