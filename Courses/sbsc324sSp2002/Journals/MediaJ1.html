Journal 1: Media Samples
Directions:  You will review five relatively brief videos aired on CNN, and produced by multimedia companies, in collaboration with Professor Mendoza.  Your task will be to view the segments in question and prepare a brief journal commentary on each.  Questions relevant to each video are noted below.  Journal entries should respond to the questions as they may or may not pertain to each video. 1.    "Time Detectives" (Scholastic, Inc., 1996): Video prepared for a primary school audience. Question:  Does the video effectively describe archaeology as per your preconceptions about what archaeologists do? 2.    "Crescent Cliff Shelter" (CNN Science and Technology Weekend Edition, December 17-18, 1994):  Video that documents the soils augering and archaeology work conducted by Professor Mendoza near Denver, Colorado. Question:  What was the stated purpose for conducting augering and soils tests of this particular site?  How does this answer questions pertinent to archaeology? 3.    "Ancient Cultures and Modern Chemistry" (Prentice-Hall, Inc., 1994):  Reviews the role of chemistry in archaeology. Question:  What role does radiocarbon dating play in the interpretation of past cultures and archaeological chronologies? 4.    "Food to Die For" (CNN Presents, November 20, 1994): Reviews a modern material cultures or "garbology" project conducted by Professor Mendoza in Denver, Colorado. Question:  What information about human behavior can be gleaned from the study of modern trash?  How can archaeology play a role in interpreting the cultures and behaviors prevalent in modern communities? 5.    "An Old Mission's Restoration" (CNN Headline News, August 22, 1998): Reviews the archaeology and restoration being conducted by Professor Mendoza and the minimum security trustees of the Gabilan Conservation Camp. Question:  How does Professor Mendoza's archaeology and museum studies work contribute to the restoration and enhancement of Old Mission San Juan Bautista?  What role do the inmates play in this restoration?  Do you believe that this project will have any impact on the lives of the trustees who participate?
