Lab Deliverable 4: Excavation Methods Review

Objectives: The purpose of the Excavation Methods Review is to test your understanding of the how the "scientific method" is deployed in the identification, layout, and excavation of archaeological sites.  Drawing upon your professors overview of his work at the Crescent Rockshelter on the Colorado Front Range, prepare to describe the basic methods, procedures, and tools used to initiate, engage, and finalize the excavations of that ancient Native American settlement.  Take careful note of the terms used to identify the methods, features, units of analysis, site characteristics, and excavation and survey units employed in such a study.

Note: Be sure to prepare your comments as a Word or simple text document that you can either post to the Online Journal before the end of class today, or save for later posting to the Online Journal.

1.    Prepare detailed notes of your professors overview of the archaeology methods employed in the excavation of the Crescent Rockshelter on the Colorado Front Range located approximately 30 miles west of Denver, Colorado.  Said notes should include those that cover both the slide presentation and the CNN (Cable News Network) broadcast of Dr. Mendoza's work on the archaeological stratigraphy of the Crescent Rockshelter.

2.    Next, break into at least four study and discussion groups, and proceed to discuss the meaning and importance to archaeology of at least ten of the following terms, tools, and concepts.  How do these terms, tools, and concepts relate to the overall scientific attempt to systematize and or organize the plan of work of an archaeological dig?  How and why are the following terms and concepts important to assuring "control" of the scientific method and the subsequent analysis of those archaeological remains recovered from such sites?

  • Research design
  • Research goals and questions
  • Sampling strategy
  • Site topography
  • Site vegetation
  • Transect or pedestrian surveys
  • Soils analysis
  • Site-level and regional maps
  • Deposition depth
  • Stratigraphy
  • Natural versus cultural formation processes
  • Recording strata
  • Datums
  • Site grid
  • Instrument maps
  • Manual proveniencing
  • Point provenience
  • Bulk or "Lot" provenience
  • Test pits
  • Trenches
  • Features
  • Unit placement
  • Excavation methods
  • Excavation tools
  • Screening
