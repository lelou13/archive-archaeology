Project-Based Learning Component

The project-based learning program of the CSUMB Institute for Archaeology is currently engaged in the development of a conservation archaeology and historic preservation plan at the Old Mission of San Juan Bautista. Project-based learning experiences will center on the the ethnohistory, and documentary archaeology of the 200 year old Spanish colonial mission of San Juan Bautista near Hollister, California. Students will work in collaboration with faculty participating in the Alta California Mission Research Project, and as such, students may pursue projects drawing on museum development and instructional technologies, historic preservation, archaeology and the hospitality industry, documentary archaeology (text-based and oral history), and field archaeology. The principal focus of the special projects section will be the development of a museum education program at San Juan Bautista coordinated between the Institute for Archaeology and the community of San Juan Bautista. While final student projects will center on the mediated development of educational exhibits and media, projects may incorporate formats ranging from the design and fabrication of museum quality educational exhibits, and or incorporate materials from typewritten journals to multi-media, virtual reality, visual anthropology, and other non-traditional approaches to archaeology and historiography.

This learning experience entails a contract-based learning plan based on outcomes and competencies evaluated through the development of an assessment portfolio or related typewritten journal. As this project entails both a lab and an off campus learning experience, students will conduct the museum exhibits installation component at San Juan Bautista, while the museum studies lab component will take place both in the field and at the CSUMB Institute for Archaeology located in Building 110 on the CSUMB campus.
