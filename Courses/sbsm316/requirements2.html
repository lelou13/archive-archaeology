Release and Waiver Forms

Note: Due to the nature of the lab and field projects that will be undertaken in this learning experience, it may be necessary from time to time to wear protection (i.e. rubber, cotton, or leather gloves, helmets, lab coats, protective goggles, respirators, etc.) and use tongs or other appropriate tools for the handling of materials. In no instance should you risk endangering yourself or other membersof the team by engaging in actions that create unnecessary risk in the lab or field -- suchas those risks resulting from the careless handlingof chemicals, equipment, and artifacts. Where lab and field exercises are concerned, you should be prepared to wear protective eye gear, rubber or cloth gloves,and respirators where necessary and or appropriate. For those that have difficulty with heat, cold or humidity, you should take the necessary precautions to avoid heat exhaustion, dehydration, or other stressors when engaged in field exercises.

Liability Release and Waiver (Hold Harmless)  A Liability Release and Waiver or Hold Harmless form is required of all students participating in this particular lab and field experience. While this is a formality, it does provide us with documentation that you have been informed of the risks of participating in the lab and field exercises of this lab and field-based learning experience.

Multimedia Release and Waiver Form  Because photographs, video, and other forms of multimedia recording may be used to document lab and field exercises, you are required to sign a Multimedia Release and Waiver Form so that said materials may be used by the instructor, staff, and students of this institution in project-based learning experiences and projects that entail multimedia development and production. See the form for further details regarding options to the rule.
