Major Deliverable 3
Internet-Based Slide Presentation
(Thematic with 10 images minimum)

Objective: To test your skills at (a) preparing images for an online presentation or instructional activity, (b) presenting digitized images within the context of a topic or theme, and (c) oral presentations with online materials.  Your objective will be to prepare between 10 and 15 digitized images for an oral presentation before your colleagues.  The online slide show should be focused on a specific topic or theme related to the social sciences.  Your presentation is to be no less than eight minutes, and no more than ten minutes, in duration.

Content:  Though content will vary from one presentation to the next, you may use online digitized images that reflect any number of different methods or approaches (e.g. object or studio photography or documentary or ethnographic photography) that reflect content drawn from within the social sciences.  If you are not a social sciences major, you may vary your subjectmatter so long as you make use of a theme that will cross-over from your particular field of study (e.g. the life and work of urban or graffiti artists).

Requirements: Digitized images should reflect a single unifying theme (e.g. student life, dorm life, migrant workers, homelessness, surfers, ravers, etc.).  The slides should constitute a theme and include (a) environmental or site shots, (b) people or object photos, (c) detail and wide shots, and (d) informational or cultural event and activity shots.  Composition, exposure, balance, format, and presentation of both digitized images and online materials will serve as key criteria in the evaluation of this deliverable.

*See Howell & Blanc (1995: 97-98; Appendix D) for some suggestions regarding "Slide Presentations."

*See your professor for the actual posting of the slide show within the context of your online portfolio.
