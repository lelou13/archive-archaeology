Studio lighting requires that the direction and intensity of light striking the subject is controlled by the photographer.  Unlike natural light, studio light provides a level of control not available to the same degree to the outdoor photographer.  In addition to building on the flatness or contrast of the image or subject, studio lighting allows for greater degrees of depth of field control and contrast.  The following diagrams are based on John Hedgecoe's book "The Photographer's Handbook" (1995).  Select the link that pertains to the sort of studio "object" lighting that you would like to explore.  These diagrams provide an illustration of the angles and types of lighting used in each situation.
