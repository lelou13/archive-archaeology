End Term Self Assessment

 

As we have finally reached the end of the semester, I have often found myself reflecting upon the classes I have taken this semester and what I have learned from them. As I so often do, and this has occured every semester during my time at CSUMB, I find myself wishing that I would have given more time to the class and learning more than what I know now. I feel that I have learned a lot about photograhy this semester, but there is always something more for me to learn.

I have found, and this has been confimed by my professor, that I have a knack for photographing plants, more specifically flowers. I love flowers and I have always loved to take pictures of the outdoors, but this semester I was able to take this pastime to another level. This new level is called Macro Photography. I think I enjoyed the Macro Photography portion of our class the most. I was able to bring in flowers and plants that were found around our lab building and be able to take such close, intimate photos of each of them. Granted, I felt major disappointment when the first two roles of film I took ended up coming back like they had been exposed to the sun for way too long (in other words, they didn't come out) but felt much relief when my second batch of pictures came back as neat and interesting as I though they would be. I do believe, though, that the digital representations of my macro photography are not as pleasing and detailed as the originals, but I feel they give the viewer an idea of what some of these most regularly seen plants and flowers look like up close.

I am also grateful for the time that we were able to spend in the lab, actually getting hands on experience taking photographs and using cameras (that I have not used before). I have always been a hands-on learner and found the lab experience to be very helpful. On the other hand, though, I do wish that we had been able to get some experience working in a darkroom, for that is one of the subjects I thought we would be learning about during the semester. But, there is always another class to learn this from.

While getting ready for the photographing portion of my Photo Essay, while using a friend's camera, I was able to learn more about cameras and how shutter speed and aperture are related to each other. I was able to see the physical results of all the effort and time that I put into the project. With the creating and designing of this web page, I was able to understand web pages and the way that photos can be presented in a web format (this was learned after making many mistakes that had to be corrected, therefor adding more hours to what I had already put into my project).

Overall, I am very happy with how I have done in this class, besides the fact that I could have put more effort into it. I have been finding it an interesting feat to be working full time and trying to juggle two classes with that. In the end, though, I think I was able to put together something very representative of the work that I did put into the class. On a final note, I would also like to thank Professor Mendoza for all the time that he worked with me, reviewing slides, giving me recommendations, and helping me decide which pictures sucked and which ones were cool beyond belief. =) Thanks.
