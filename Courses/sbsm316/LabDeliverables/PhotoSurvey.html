The Photographic Survey:
Identifying the Elements of a Photographic Survey
    The Photographic Survey
       
    Instructions: After completing with our review of media images drawn from popular magazines and journals and American media in general, we will devote the second half of the class to reviewing a photographic exhibition of Professor Mendoza's work located in CSUMB Building 49 & 48 (World Languages and Cultures Buildings).

    As with the media review, you should look to identifying that information available from the review of visual content within the photographs exhibited in building 49. Whereas the media review sought to identify such information as overall environment, people, composition, perspective, etc., you should in this instance examine the exhibition photographs for the following details, evidence, and information:

    1. location
    2. appearance
    3. organization
    4. functions
    5. people
    6. transportation
    7. daily cycles
    8. history
    9. change.

    Prepare to identify what it is within the composition of any one photo that lends itself to identifying these basic aspects of visual information. Be prepared to ask yourself what it is within a specific photo or photos that lends itself to informing the viewer about the above aspects of a cultural scene.
