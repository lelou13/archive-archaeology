Course Description

SBS 260L/360L: Applied Archaeology Lab - 4.0 credits [CRN 21130 / 21131]

Description: Provides hands-on training in archaeology methods and procedures. Includes readings and applied learning in which students undertake a specific analytical or experimental procedure that makes use of archaeological materials recovered in the field. Content may vary depending on student interests and project schedules. Covers basic procedures for archaeological fieldwork, and the use of archaeological technologies. Includes artifact collections and specimens analysis; mapping and interpretation; faunal analysis and bone identification; ceramic and lithic analysis; and collections management.

Co-Requisite: The Applied Archaeology Lab is the necessary co-requisite for the SBS 260s/360s (Archaeology of a California Mission) lab and field offering offered each year. In order to receive full credit for the SBS 260s/360s field course, you must co-enroll in, and successfully complete, the SBS 260L/360L Applied Archaeology Lab.

University Learning Requirements: This lab is the necessary co-requisite for addressing both the upper division (SBS 360s) Service Learning and SCIENCE (Methods) ULR requirements at CSU Monterey Bay. This lab offering is in turn required of SBS Archaeology majors who require fulfillment of the Major Learning Outcome in Theory (MLO 1), Methods (MLO 2), and Applied (MLO 3) or Area concentrations.

Note: If you require Service Learning credit, please note that you should be registered in the upper division, SBS 360s and 360L sections of this program offering. Lower division or introductory Community Participation (SL) credit is offered primarily through the Service Learning Institute at CSU Monterey Bay.

Note: During the Spring 2003 term the Institute for Archaeology received a grant from a congressional appropriation through the US Department of Education so as to conduct a Wireless Technologies in Archaeology demonstration project. This offering of SBS 260s/360s serves as a demonstration project site for wireless technologies. As such, students will be trained in the use of wireless Pocket or Tablet PC's and related technologies, and you will make active use such information management tools during the course of lab and field outings in order to prepare Online Journal and or Discussion Forum entries. For further information on this unique and significant project, please see http://archaeology.csumb.edu/wireless/
