Lab Precautions


Lab and Field Project Conditions

Given the nature of archaeological lab and field efforts, it should be assumed by all project participants that certain precautions should be observed so as to minimize potential hazards and the risk entailed in failing to follow prescribed procedures. The most obvious risks concern failing to observe basic or common sense considerations regarding physical exertion and exposure to the elements. The presence of open excavation units, guidewires or grids, excavation tools and equipment, sharp or jagged specimens, dehydration, exposure to the sun, and related considerations should always be kept in mind at all times. So as to minimize exposure to some of these hazards, the following recommendations are deemed critical to your participation.

  • Project participants are encouraged to wear suitable eye and hand protection when engaged in activities where appropriate.
  • Avoid lifting heavy boxes or objects without the proper assistance, equipment, or supervision.
  • Never stand on balk or side walls immediately adjacent open excavation units as these may collapse under your weight.
  • Always observe care in walking within or about excavation or processing areas as open units or hazardous debris (such as glass shards) may be present.
  • Exercise caution in wielding hand picks, shovels, mattocks, trowels, and related tools in the presence of other project participants.
  • Never fill available buckets to the brim as this is a violation of OSHA standards, and may result in injury to your back or strained muscles and tendons.
  • Use gloves to skim, sift, or cull specimens from the sieves or sifting screens, particularly as glass shards, wire, nails, or other sharp objects may be present.
  • In sum, be ever vigilant of any and all potential hazards that may arise from working in and about ancient buildings, excavation features, or uneven flooring and related surfaces.

Multimedia Release and Waiver: Because some aspects of lab project efforts may be filmed and or photographed, you may be asked to sign a multimedia or photo release provided prior to the beginnings of lab and field work, or project tours, undertaken on campus or in off-campus settings. If you choose not to be included in any filming or photography, please indicate this fact on your signed multimedia release. If you choose not to be photographed, it will be your responsibility to notify any and all photographers, including other student photographers, of this fact at the time that photographs are being taken. It will also be your responsibility to remove yourself from areas being photographed or filmed at any and all times through the course of the semester.

Liability Release and Waiver: All student and volunteer project participants are required to fully complete and sign a project liability release and waiver prior to participation in the archaeological lab and field program of the CSU Monterey Bay Institute for Archaeology. This assures us that you fully understand the possible risks and or hazards that may accompany any type of lab or field exercise. In addition to those forms provided by your professor, the Service Learning Institute requires that you complete and sign a Service Learning agreement that is distributed prior to off-campus service or project-related activities.
