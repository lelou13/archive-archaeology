Primary Readings


Primary readings or texts selected for this course have been selected for the purposes of serving as Applied Archaeology Lab guides to project procedures and discussion forums and deliverables. Readings from the Sutton and Arkush (2002) volume provide that basic level of familiarity required to participate in lab activities. Said text will serve as the source for Reader Response essays required through the term. The Ayres (1995) volume provides but one point of departure for exploring the archaeology of Spanish and Mexican colonialism from an interdisciplinary perspective. In addition, Ayers (1995) will serve as the default text for the Midterm Reader Response, and in turn, necessarily constitutes required content for the final lab report.

Note: Additional readings may serve to supplement primary text materials, and will include both Reserve Desk readings, Internet reports, and related sources. Final project reports will require that you reflect your readings on Spanish and Mexican colonialism in your written narratives, as well as your understanding of basic archaeology lab procedures. Although the final lab report will largely encompass those observations, and methods and procedures undertaken during the project investigation, your introductory narratives and bibliography should in fact reflect additional source materials.

 

Required Lab Texts

Sutton, Mark Q., and Brooke S. Arkush.  2002. Archaeological Laboratory Methods: An Introduction. Third Edition. Dubuque, Iowa: Kendall/Hunt Publishing Company.  ISBN:0-7872-8153-0.

Ayres, James E. (Compiler).  1995.  The Archaeology of Spanish and Mexican Colonialism in the American Southwest, Guides to the Archaeological Literature of the Immigrant Experience in America, No. 3, The Society for Historical Archaeology.  ISBN: 1-886818-01-0.
