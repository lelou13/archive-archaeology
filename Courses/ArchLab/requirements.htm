Assessment/Grades

It should be noted that the basis for grading and assessment of this section might be subject to slight variation from what is noted below in the event that the class shifts its emphasis for the purposes of study options and those projects undertaken. Any changes will be subject to class discussion and consensus where appropriate.

Participation: Because this Applied Archaeology Lab will require your participation in lab exercises (such as that of Mission Ceramics) that require documentation of said participation, your regular use of the Discussion Forum will constitute 25 percentage points of your final grade. This will serve to document your direct attendance and participation. Participation will in part be assessed by way of the timely and thorough completion of (a) Discussion Forum or Wireless Journal entries completed on a weekly basis, and (b) the documentation of in-class Lab Deliverables (see below) via the Discussion Forum and through the submission of supporting class materials related to the Lab Deliverables (25 points).

Note: Because many of the online and wireless journal entries will be completed during class time, and will address class participation and lab exercises, it is critical that your participation be reflected in the Online and or Wireless Journal. In fact, your professor uses online and wireless journal entries to assess credit for participation.

Discussion Forum: http://archaeology.csumb.edu/phpbb2

Wireless Journals: http://wireless_archaeology.csumb.edu/

Finally, please note that much of what we cover in weekly labs will require you to bring to each class both of the required texts as each of these works contain much of the reference material that we will require for said labs.

Futures Lab Field Journal: The Futures Lab field journal will require you to obtain a small science or lab notebook, available through the campus bookstore. It is here that you should record questions that arise regarding readings, discussions, and lab deliverables on an ongoing basis. Your questions should be detailed, carefully thought through, and coherent to the extent that someone other than yourself should be able to decipher what it is that you had in mind at any given point of the semester. You should include hand-rendered diagrams or illustrations of your ideas or observations pertaining to the questions, where appropriate, as one other way of presenting your information. The Futures Lab field journal will be submitted, along with your Final Portfolios, as one other measure of your participation in in-class labs and discussion group projects (see Participation for credit).

Reader Response Papers: Approximately five typewritten (one page) reader response papers will be completed by you and submitted in hardcopy as single-spaced narratives that provide sufficient detail so that the instructor can assess your thorough completion and understanding of the lab readings assigned. The reader response papers will account for approximately 30 percentage points toward your semester grade. The reader response papers will be graded at 3 points each per minimum one-page essay if they demonstrate your thorough understanding and reflection of the readings. Two points will be assessed if a partial understanding of the readings are reflected, and one percentage point will be assessed if the essay was completed but did not reflect a clear understanding of the readings assigned (30 percentage points possible).


Midterm Book Review: A brief midterm book review will be required as one other point of departure for your instructor’s assessment of your performance at midterm. In this instance, the primary text reading for midterm consideration may be drawn from a list of at least five books from a proposed bibliography prepared by you and submitted in advance for approval by your professor. In order to receive credit for the midterm book review, you will need to show that your professor approved the bibliography submitted by you prior to submission of the midterm book review itself. Otherwise, the second required course text (Ayers) will remain the default text for review this term (15 points).

Note: You may forego the Midterm Book Review through the submission of an additional five one-page reader response papers, for a total of ten Reader Response papers for the term. The text chapters and or scholarly articles that you review will be selected in consultation with your professor beforehand, or may be of your own choosing from existing class resources; however, for those articles that you select from beyond existing resources, you must obtain approval from your professor prior to preparing reviews of articles not assigned in class.

Endterm Self Assessment: At end term each class participant will submit a self-assessment statement in which he or she responds to a series of questions about one's overall performance to that respective point in the semester. The questionnaire for this exercise may be found at: http://archaeology.csumb.edu/Guidelines/Questionnaires/SelfAssessment.pdf (10 points).

Final Lab Report: Your end term objective is to submit a final Lab Report (of no less than 5 typewritten double-spaced pages not including bibliography and illustrations) with content based on your semester Applied Archaeology Lab efforts (see Format Guidelines at Projects). Only that data available from past or current archaeological field investigations may be used as the basis for developing your descriptive lab report. The Final Lab Report is worth 20 percentage points towards your final grade (20 points).

· Participation (Discussion Forum / Wireless Journal Entries): 30
· Reader Response Papers (5 @ 3 pts. each): 15
· Midterm Book Review (Approved Bibliography): 15
· Endterm Self Assessments (5 pts.): 5
· Final Lab Report (See Lab Report Guidelines): 20
· Final Portfolio with Table of Contents: 15

· Optional UCSC or UC Berkeley Research Lab Tour (5 pts.): Total possible - 10

Total Possible Points: 110 pts.
