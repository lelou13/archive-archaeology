Note: This schedule is subject revision as per class interests and needs. Reader Response papers (RR) and Lab (LD) and Major Deliverable (MD) due dates for SBS 260s/360s are noted below. See Reader Response format at http://archaeology.csumb.edu/Guidelines/FormatGuides/sbs324sF03RR3Sample.pdf. Reader Response papers are not to exceed one typewritten single-spaced page per Unit or Chapter assignment.

Discussion Forums: For the SBS 260L/360L (Archaeology Lab) Required Lab Readings, you are to review each chapter noted below, and submit three questions -- via the relevant week's Discussion Forum folder -- that pertain to the readings for the given week. Questions should reflect thoughtful consideration of the text chapter content and or that week's lab discussions.

Please consult the online version of this schedule on an ongoing basis in order to get updates on changes that may accrue. From time to time, your professor will make revisions on the evening before, or the morning of, class meetings so as to make revisions necessitated by student participation and course progress.

The hard copy and electronic submission of all class deliverables completed to date will be required at both midterm and end term, and should be delivered to your professor via electronic 3.5” diskette or CD copy. Otherwise, all deliverables must be submitted in hardcopy portfolio format for assessment by the specified deadlines noted in the schedule below.

See http://archaeology.csumb.edu/Guidelines/AssessmentGuides/PortfolioAssessmentFormFnl.pdf for a sample of (final) Portfolio Assessment Criteria.


Co-Requisite: This Schedule essentially duplicates that for SBS 260s/360s (Archaeology of a California Mission). Because the SBS 260L/360L (Archaeology Lab) is the co-requisite of SBS 260s/360s, activities and deliverables here will mirror those assigned in the Field portion of this offering. The Archaeology Lab will provide a venue within which you may address Lab Requirements for the SBS 260s/360s (Archaeology of a California Mission) Field offering. The primary exception, regarding requirements in the SBS 260L/360L Lab offering, concerns those readings derived from the Lab texts. In order to receive full credit for SBS 260s/360s, you must successfully complete SBS 260L/360L as well. See SBS 260s/360s syllabus at http://archaeology.csumb.edu/Courses/MissionArchaeology/introduction.htm

Archaeology Lab Hours: Lab time is available on-site or in the field at the culmination of the Friday field day, or on the option Lab Day normally scheduled for Thursday afternoons. Typically, the Lab consists of the (a) processing field specimens excavated on any given field day, (b) data collection and information gathering, and the conduct of exercises pertaining to Lab Deliverables. We will, in addition, sponsor at least two Lab Days on those days normally assigned to Field Days (See Below).
