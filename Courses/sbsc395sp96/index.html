Technical Imaging Applications

SBSC 395 - Section 1A

A Virtual Learning Lab in the Social Sciences

Professor Ruben G. Mendoza, Ph.D.

Institute of Archaeology
Social and Behavioral Sciences Center

Learning Experience Description

A project-based technical imaging lab and field experience that spans the use of photographic techniques as data collection technologies in archaeological, ethnographic, and sociological contexts. Students will engage in the hands-on production of photo-technical imaging appropriate to application, interpretation, and analysis in the social and behavioral sciences. Project-based technical skills development will encompass selected concentrations in studio photography or controlled lighting, museum, artifact, and product photography, historic photograph collections and their conservation, field-based photographic techniques and image interpretation, infrared applications and aerial photography interpretation, basic darkroom photographic processing, and micro-videography and micro-photographic technical applications in the social and behavioral sciences.
