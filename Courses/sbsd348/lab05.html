Lab Deliverable 5

Interpreting the Panel of the 96 Glyphs

Description: As an initial foray into the world of Maya glyph interpretation and translation, we will begin with a basic assignment that makes use of a flashcard set in order to conduct an interpretation of a specific Maya monument from the site of Palenque, Chiapas, Mexico. The following instructions will serve to guide you through the process of interpreting the Maya monument in question.

1. Break into your pre-assigned group or team. If you do not have a team designation, or are in a group too small to perform, please join the members of the next smallest team.

2. Having come together, each team member should be assigned a key role in interpreting the monument in question. Because we will be working in three languages (mainly, Maya, Spanish, English), and the interpretation itself is part of a group effort, each team member should have an assigned task (e.g. interpretation, notetaking, translation, etc.).

3. Using the glyph handouts distributed last week, including the handout of the monument itself, first interpret, and thereby, predict, the direction and format of the translation that you will undertake.

4. Prior to translating the monument, each team member should provide a one paragraph description of what they believe the monument has to say about the site of Palenque, Chiapas. You should be prepared to submit this proposed interpretation at the end of class today.

5. Having completed your paragraph-length description of your thoughts on the monument, each group will be provided with a portion of a larger set of flash cards with Maya glyphs. Using the flash cards, match, identify, and transcribe that portion of text on the monument panel that you have been assigned. Please bear in mind that the cards are translated from Maya to English.

6. Prepare your portion of the transcription, and for those terms that may or may not translate directly, please use the Maya-Spanish dictionary with translations provided under your instructors guidance.

7. Please bear in mind that there is no right or wrong answer in this Lab Deliverable, and all sections transcribed will be written out on paper and one person from your group should forward (by e-mail) the Maya-English transcription, along with Group or Team Color or Name, and the names of all participants involved in today's exercise.
