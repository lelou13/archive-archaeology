Lab Deliverable 3

Traditional versus Modern Views of the Maya

Description: Drawing on your readings from the Sabloff text, chapters 1 through 3, please come to class next week prepared to respond in writing to the following questions.

1. How does Sabloff characterize the nature of archaeological research among the Maya? In other words, what did the archaeologists of the new school of archaeology (the New Archaeologists) demand of studies and research among the Maya? (Chapter 1)
 
 

2. Why does Sabloff refer to the New Archaeology as "modern scientific archaeology"? (Chapter 1)
 
 

3. Be prepared to present at least three distinct features of comparison for how the traditional approach to the Maya differs from the new and improved modern version of archaeology among the Maya. In other words, what are the primary differences you see as significant between "Traditional" versus "Modern" archaeology? (Chapter 2)
 
 

4. How would you distinguish the work and interpretations of the "Victorian" age archaeologists from those of Sir Eric Thompson's generation? (Chapter 2)
 
 

5. Sabloff devotes considerable attention to the question of Maya settlement patterns. Why are settlement pattern studies important to our understanding of the Maya? How did the settlement studies of the 1950's through 1980's change our views of Maya populations and agricultural practices? (Chapter 3)
