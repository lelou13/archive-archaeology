Major Deliverable 2

The Proposed Term Project

Description: Please prepare a preliminary proposal regarding your planned term project. All proposals should be in typewritten format, and no less than three pages in length. All pages should be numbered, each section of your paper should be subtitled (e.g. "Conclusions"), and each page of the paper should include your name and page number in the upper right-hand corner. All proposals should include the following sections and adhere to the following requirements.

1. Introduction: a. Identify the specific issue that will be treated by your paper or related project.

b. Provide background information that will allow the reader to judge the importance of the work being undertaken.

c. Specify the approach that you have chosen to undertake and why you have chosen the specific approach noted.

d. Specify or advance at least two or three key questions that relate to your specific area of study. 1. Said questions should serve to guide the narrative content.

2. Said questions should provide a basis for the response posed in the conclusions section of your paper.


2. Narrative: a. Provide a statement that narrows the scope of the specific research that you have chosen to undertake.

b. Provide details and a narrative overview of the specific facts minimally required for the reader to understand your overall narrative. 1. who, what, where, when, why, and how

2. description of terms and localities or themes explored c. Provide a narrative of the case and facts that will serve to underscore or support your specific view of the Maya world and the specific theme that you have chosen to undertake.

d. Be sure that your narrative actually addresses the issues or specific questions that you raised at the outset -- particularly those specific to the introductory statement.


3. Conclusions: a. Restate, and respond to, the questions or problems (or thesis) posed at the outset of your paper.

b. State, or restate, those conclusions drawn as the result of your research into the questions, problems, or thesis statements made at the outset of your paper.

c. Avoid using a quote from someone else's work to summarize or conclude your paper.


4. References Cited: a. Please cite a minimum of five (5) references beyond those specific to your readings in this class. 1. In other words, you should cite Sabloff (1990), Schele and Freidel (1990), and Coe (1992), and at least five other books, journals or scholarly articles.

2. For this preliminary review, I will not consider Internet sites as valid references, although I will accept a percentage of your final project references in the form of Internet sites URL addresses. b. Please provide a two sentence annotation of the reference cited for your preliminary proposal.

c. Scholarly books, academic journals, or high quality magazines (like National Geographic or Archaeology Magazine) are the only acceptable reference sources for this preliminary proposal.


5. Electronic Format:

Though the foregoing sections are required of the preliminary proposal, particularly those related to written term paper projects, you will prepare a basic web portfolio or related electronic document as a final contribution to the class project. The aforementioned guidelines should be adhered to in any written proposal submitted so as to fulfill the requirements of Major Deliverable 2.
