Lab Deliverable 2

Assessing Media Images of the Maya

Description: This day's learning experience will center on the review of a video concerned with the ancient and modern Maya.  Entitled, "Central America: The Burden of Time," this video is part of a six part series entitled Legacy concerned with the influence of the past on modern societies. Before airing this video, you will be given the opportunity to review the video "Maya: The Blood of Kings" so as to provide a comparative basis on which to evaluate the influence and nature of media attention on the Maya.

Please be prepared to address the following questions; break into group discussion, and document some of your observations regarding the role of the media in forming your perspectives on the Maya and other ancient Native American groups and peoples.

1. Do you feel that the presentation is balanced and or biased? If not, how would you change the video and its content so as to address what you feel to be the real issues of concern?

2. What does the video suggest about the link between the ancient and modern Maya? How does the theme of "The Burden of Time" fit into the overall presentation? How does this theme fit into the video segment on the "Blood of Kings"?

3. What impressions did you take from this film regarding the ways in which the Maya have been transformed since pre-Columbian times?

4. How does the metaphor of the "shaman" concerned with time and the counting of the days in a modern Maya village (ala "Burden of Time") connect or link to the counting of the days in the ancient Maya calendrical system? Do you feel that the "count of the days" in the ancient Long Count system of notation have anything to do with the continued count of the days among the Maya? If so, how so? If not, why not?

5. Given what the video suggests about the Maya and their respective cultural and social traditions, what do you believe might have become of the Maya had their civilization not collapsed as it did in the 10th Century A.D.?

6. To what extent did the respective videos change your preconceptions about the Maya civilization and its people?
