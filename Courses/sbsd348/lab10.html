Lab Deliverable 10

Oral Presentation & Project Overview

Description: Your final Lab Deliverable for this course will require a brief -- five to eight minute -- oral presentation and project overview of that study undertaken by you for the purposes of your term project. So that we can best coordinate the presentations within the parameters of that time available to us (see Class Schedule), you will conduct your presentation from within the context of your normally assigned group.

1. For the purposes of coordinating your topics into broader "themes," please break into your pre-assigned group or team. If you do not have a team designation, or you are in a group too small to collaborate effectively, please join the members of the next smallest team. If the other members of your assigned team have chosen topics widely different than your own, you may wish to join another team.

2. Having formed your team, each member of each team should identify their respective oral presentation topic or group theme, and each group should then decide on the order of the presentations to be delivered by each team member during next week's final class session.

3. The first or last presenter of each group will serve as the monitor or moderator of each presentation. The moderator is to monitor the amount of time used by each presenter, and is to signal to the presenter when they have only one minute remaining for their respective time limit. Because we may run short on time for presentations if time limits are not strictly enforced, the moderator's role is critical. Each moderator will coordinate the creation of a list of topics and presenters and submit said list by the end of today's session.

4. The moderator chosen for your respective group should collect all group names and topics in that order in which said topics will be presented. Your speaker's list should include the following: (a) moderator's name, (b) group theme, (c) presenter's name by numbered order of presentation, (d) presenter's topic, and (e) audiovisual needs.

5. Please be prepared to start your group's presentations at the outset of the time allotted (i.e., no later than 5:10 pm). Out of respect for your peers, it is advised that each team's members remain for the duration of the class presentations. The ordering of the groups themselves will be determined by lots (i.e., "a flip of the coin" or "straws").

6. Good Luck!!!
