Author: Cynthia Tiberend. - Subject: Excavation Day1

Date: 9/15/99. - Time: 2:55:50 AM. - Entry Num.: 218

Today, was my first day at the San Juan Bautista site. Professor Mendoza gave the Orientation. I excavated in the N10 W38 NW1/4 Quarter, level 1 area. My observations of the site was very interesting. Presently, the area that is being excavated, is where a former building stood, before raiding Indians burned it down. I learned how to excavate with different types of tools. Excavating the site is tedious and dirty work, but leaning the process was worth the effort. Proper excavating techniques were introduced to us, and we how to search for artifacts through either wet or dry methods. First, I dug up enough dirt and material in the quarter, and then I shifted the dirt with the wet (water) method to look for artifacts. I found the following artifacts: reddish-brown building materials, stone tool chert flakes, bottle caps, larger stone tools that were used to grind food, pottery shards. Another student I had been working with, found some old Indian beads. I am looking forward to learning more about the History associated with Archaeology. I have an interest in History.

Author: Cynthia Tiberend. - Subject: San Juan Bautista Mission Field Work #4

Date: 10/6/99. - Time: 12:41:27 AM. - Entry Num.: 307
October 1,1999
Today, I went out to San Juan Bautista Mission for my fourth field work project. The weather was great as usual!! I worked in the N10W38SE1/4 quarter. John and I worked together to bring the level down to 40cm. Presently, we are at level two. I found this quarter to be easier to dig. The dirt wasn't as hard and didn't contain as much adobe. This site was exciting to work in because I found a lot of interesting artifacts. I had
 to use the pick ax more carefully than before. Because we found large bone and a rusty  wire that was embedded in tile material. I was curious to find out where this wire was leading to. Finally, I uncovered the rest of the remaining wire, it wasn't attached any other
 artifacts. The artifacts I found were: metal tool, rusty wire, charcoal, stucco, and black transferware (ceramic) siltsone, and tile fragments. The most exciting artifact I found that day was the ceramic transferware because the detail was beautiful. The pattern consisted of small white-brown flowers. Also, I learned more about how to catalog artifacts insitu within the site. John also found some good artifacts, such as white opalized glass      fragments.

Author: Cynthia Tiberend. - Subject: San Juan Bautista Mission Excavation Day 5

Date: 11/10/99. - Time: 2:05:55 AM. - Entry Num.: 139
I haven't been up to the excavation site, for a couple of weeks. So, I have been looking forward to finish working in my unit. Professor Mendoza suggested that I continue working in the N10W38SE1/4. The objective is still 40cm. I worked with John in the unit, we were
able to reach the third level. Before we started to excavate further, we had to dismantle the first and second levels that we had been digging around because they had been disturbed. At one point, we were exciting because we thought we had found a human skull or a canon ball!! However, our hopes we crushed because Professor Mendoza determined that the artifact was a polished rock with pressure cracks running through it. I used the dry sifting method instead of the wet method. The artifacts I found were red building material, white earthenware ceramic fragments, stucco, and adobe building  material. Unfortunately, I didn't find anything exciting. Although, Professor Mendoza believes we are getting closer to the building wall, we have been excavating for.

Author: Cynthia Tiberend. - Subject: San Juan Bautista Day II

 Date: 9/22/99. - Time: 1:55:28 AM. - Entry Num.: 237
Today, September 17,1999, I went to work at San Juan Bautista which is part of my course work. After my first visit a week ago, I have become more comfortable and knowledgeable about what I am doing. Thus far, the excavation at the site, has been very interesting. Yet, the excavating itself has been slow and tedious. I find myself wanting to dig faster. so that I can find good artifacts. Last Friday, I excavated in the same quarter
(N10 W38 NW1/4,Level 1) where I continued to dig to reach 20cm. While I was excavating, I found small ceramic fragments and some different colored glass fragments, which I wasv very exciting. Professor Mendoza taught me how to square off the sides of the N10 W38
NW1/4 quarter. I learned how to use more archaeological tools. The tools I used to square the sides with were pick axes, small picks, measuring rope, and a level. When I found the artifacts, I learned how to document where the ceramic (artifact) is located in the site. Then I had to write and document how much the artifact weighed, length, width, etc...Then I recorded the information onto the catalog card. The artifact went into the bag with the catalog card. Finally, I obtained a better technique and knowledge of how to wet/dry shift the excavation dirt. Besides the ceramic, colored glass, I found more building material, bone and wood. While I was excavating, Dr. Mendoza's tour group came through. So we
had to look like we knew what we were doing, oh well...I would like to take his tour sometime to learn more about San Juan Bautista, even though I have toured the site many times.
