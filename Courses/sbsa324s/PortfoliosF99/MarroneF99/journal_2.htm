The lost spanish mission was led by David Hurst Thomas and his crew of coliques. They used a transect sampling strategy where they would walk west and east transects which measured 100 meteres wide. In every site they doug two or more 1 meter square units ( which were test units ) and as many as 400 pits. With a gas powered posthole digger they were able to excavate hundreds of holes which were about 3 feet deep. Power augers were also used to throw dirt up for sampling. Another tool that was used was a proton procession magnetometer which measures the magnetism between the earths magnetic core and the sensor on the machine. These tools increased the teams productivity tremendously. It gave faster and more acurate results which saves time and money. Not only did it do these things but they used tools that would not hurt the environment in order for it to be preserved for future generations. Thomas states that conceptualizing and rationalizing the research area is why they they dig sites. Not only that but it gives the team an good idea as to how objects found are related to each other. Middle range research is made up of Ethnoarchaeology, garbology, and experimental archaeology. To be an ethnoarchaeologist you must live amongst the people you are studying and make assumptions about the people who used to live there. Garbology is the study of peoples trash. By this you can tell everything to a persons diet to their sexual activities. Experimental archaeology is doing things in such a way that it was believed to have been done in the past according to gathered data.
