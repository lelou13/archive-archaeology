Video Review

The primary myths of archaeology in North America were that the native people were too savage and barbaric to be responsible for building the mounds. One of the beliefs was that the Indians killed off the moundbuilders. Even when it was proven that the Indians were the same race as the mound builders it was still hard for people to dismiss their racist beliefs, in order to keep exploiting them and taking their land. Hunter and gatherers were most successful and had much free time to engage in other activities such as art.  The invention of agriculture had the same effects on civilization here as it did elsewhere. I think salvage archaeology is important in that it can at least give us some insight into our past before it is completely destroyed and in some cases give us cause to re-evaluate development and even halt it or slow it down.

LD:Divine Mission Video

I think it is important to restore the artwork and architecture to its original state. I would probably make restoration a priority along with education in order to gain interest and support for the project. Art should be restored to the original whenever possible. Newer versions are noteworthy and deserve mention only. San Juan Bautista relies a lot on tourism, so the main focus should probably be on restoring those things that people want to see such as artwork, buildings, and museums. Things that are in danger of being lost or ruined should come first.
