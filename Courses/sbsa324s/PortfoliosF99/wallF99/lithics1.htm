This was a difficult project since I only have one set of data from the lithics lab. The section that I chose and documented was a very large sample and full of debitage. This is the reason I only had time for one data set.

I think that since I didn't have a category for the four to five centimeter range, that I may have included those in the "over five" category. I spent a lot of time trying to present this data in a logical way. It was not easy dealing with the different measurements.

In the less than one centimeter category, I had a lot of dust and very small particles that I just couldn't count, yet they are represented in the overall weight of the category. I had measured and counted my samples before we changed the sizes to reflect five to seven range and over seven. Therefore everything in my over five section could include quite a variety, although there were only six items in that category, and none of them were over seven centimeters long. The biggest variation for weight and quantity was in the smallest and the largest ranges for weight and number of items. The middle ranges were more equal in weight and quantity. I think if I were better at Excel, I would better be able to represent the data. I am not sure how representative my data is since I think I may have made an error and I only had one set to look at. I find all of this very interesting and would like to explore it further.
