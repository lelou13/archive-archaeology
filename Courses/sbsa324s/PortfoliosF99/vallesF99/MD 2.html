INTRODUCTION
As this research was underway, a question arose and followed me throughout.  What was life like for the Indigenous before the Missionaries arrived in central Monterey?
Life was shaped in a totally different manner before the 1700's when exposure to the Spanish occurred.  I will discuss in a more thorough manner what the Native American perspective was like, and how they viewed the encounter and what was going on during the tome of the cultural genocide of their people.
Just as well, I will touch upon the Missionary's perspective on the "enlightening" of their arrival.  What they felt they were doing, as opposed to what they actually did.
I will discuss a period in time when nature and familism were the core of a society and what levels of organization existed, how it deteriorated though time and what the Missionary's role in that was.
I will elaborate on what life like was, making the traditional and established tribes such as Esselen and Salinan my focus, and San Juan Bautista my focal mission.  I am making those tribes my focal tribes for the purpose of linking the Indians who would have inhabited San Juan Bautista.  I want to bring this research paper to a more personal and local level, and tying it to San Juan Bautista for purposes of tying it to the archaeological excavations, I have participated in at that particular mission.
I want, through this research paper, uncover how what one perspective on a particular action can have drastic unexpected and unintentional results.

THESIS
Economics and the push of religion were the main forces behind the colonization of the Native Indigenous people in Alta. California.  The result was a colonization of mind, body and spirit.  The ultimate result was genocide.  What I hope to discuss is the life before Spanish Inquisition for the Native Indians, the intentions of the Franciscan Priests and what the goal of establishing missions was.
In addition, I will include how the existence of missions resulted in acculturation.

REVIEW OF LITERATURE
What I have found through the literature I have used for my research has been the concept of consistancy when applied to the exposure of missions upon the Indians.  Consistancy because many times the reasoning behind the establishment of missions was for the spread of Christianity which came hand in hand with civilizing.  One of the most disturbing quotes that I came to upon completing my research was one discussing the awareness of acculturation that the Franciscan Priests carried with them.  They were concious of the impact they were going to have on the Indians, and if anything that was the goal, to destruct and eliminate the existance of a society and the beliefs they carried, simply because they felt that what they carried as a society was the right way.  They felt it was the beliefs any civilized and educated people should believe in their God, without considering how much they would change and destroy on their quest for "saving savages."  One of the primary objecties of the Franciscan directed mission programs in Alta. California was the transformation of the culture and world view of the Indian converts congregated in the missions
