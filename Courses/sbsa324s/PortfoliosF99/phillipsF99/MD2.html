MAJOR DELIVERABLE 2

    Nancy Phillips
    Major Deliverable 2
    SBSC 224
    Fall 1999
     

    In the five days I have spent so far in the Lithics Lab, I have experienced a variety of activities and received a multitude of information regarding stone tools, their creation and study. These included trial flintknapping and the tools involved, the gathering of debitage left by the flintknapping along with its precise documentation, further workings with obsidian after having read a great deal of material on the subject via handouts, and a self-guided experimentation day in which I spent time working with a piece of obsidian in a trial-and-error method to observe flaking and breaking of the piece, much like a Neanderthal may have done when he or she first approached the problem of creating tools for survival. I found that my skill level is not very high and this activity does not come naturally to me. However, I plan to spend more time in the lab with some pieces I have labeled for myself as well as attempt a few more pieces. I am improving with each lab meeting.
    I have been keeping notes in a notebook so that I can use them later to present charts and summaries of the activities for my final project. By attempting to create stone tools in the lab, an archaeological site is being created by the lab participants. In my project, I will attempt to interpret the data collected and determine various conclusions to this study, based on what I have learned in this course.
    I have requested several books on the subject of stone tool analysis. I think these materials will open the doors to new interpretations and analyses for me since this is a relatively new perspective of looking at stone tools for me.
