Major Deliverable 1

Nancy Phillips
Major Deliverable 1
SBSC 224
Fall 1999

1. The reason I enrolled in this class was to complete my science ULR. The subject of archaeology appealed to me because I love working with land and knowing its history. I lived in Carmel Valley for five years and did landscaping on the property. The owner has found many mortars and pestles. I still work out there when I can. I would be participating in the excavation project, but I have a commitment every Friday this semester. The title “From Map to Museum” also had a special appeal to me. I worked for several years at the Monterey Museum of Art and I thought there might also be some connection to a museum with this course. Although this turned out not to be the case, the course content has been quite engaging. I am glad I took this course.

2. I have attended all class meetings and participated in all Lab and Field Deliverables so far. My commitment and involvement with them has grown as the semester has progressed.

3. I feel that my responses on the Online Journal have improved since the beginning of the course, due to my becoming more comfortable with the material and the format of the course. I am more satisfied with the later responses.

4. So far, I have been exploring the topic of flintknapping for my final project. My first attempt at the actual craft was not very successful, but I have since read the handouts and explored some websites. However, I would like to know more of what’s entailed in the final report and what the Garbology Project involves before I commit myself to one subject. I intend to use the Lithics Lab as the subjectmatter of my final report. I need clarification as to what is entailed in the final report.

5. See above.

6. I found Lab Deliverable 5 to be interesting. I enjoyed following the process that David Hurst Thomas and his crew used to narrow their search for Santa Catalina de Guale Mission on St. Catherine’s Island. Remote sensing, in particular, intrigued me as I watched how they arrived at their conclusion. The Material Culture Two exercise also interested me. I was fascinated by the detail of the symbols we looked at on the codex. A third concept I found important was introduced in the Chichen Itza lecture. In the past, man has theorized using his own assumptions about what he sees and cannot understand. We now see that Le Plongeon used European assumptions when he encountered ancient Mayan cities. I feel this is one of the most important concepts to keep in mind as I attempt a project: to constantly question my assumptions.

7. The Lab Deliverable I enjoyed most was the Video Review of the video about the mounds. I used to visit an Indian mound near my house in Ohio regularly. I have always felt intrigued by the mounds. Again, in the video, assumptions inhibited the search for the answer to who built the mounds until Cyrus Thomas established a link between Indians and the mounds. The Material Cultures 3 Lab perplexed me. I felt I didn’t have prior knowledge about different types of stone. I didn’t know how to describe it and really couldn’t envision what I was looking at.

8. For the remainder of the semester, I plan to pay closer attention to detail and get clarification of what is expected in the assignments. So far, my strengths have been digesting the written material by taking the time to read and comprehend it. However, I appreciate the time in the lab to get assignments done right away as they can easily get piled up.
