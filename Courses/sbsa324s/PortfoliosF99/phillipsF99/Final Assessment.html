Final Self Assessment

Nancy Phillips
End Term Self Assessment
SBSC 1999
Archaeology: From Map to Museum
Fall 1999

In addition to fulfilling a ULR, my objectives for this course, as with any course I enroll in are always to learn valuable information I can use in my daily life as well as my course of study. This course accomplished that for me. I found a lot of the material fascinating and inspiring.

I was committed to being actively involved in the Lithics Lab experiment and attend all class meetings and Lab days. In this way, I was able to keep with the pace of the experiment and not miss any important details. As a result, I learned a lot, had an abundance of information and resources to work with in my Final Report, and felt a sense of accomplishment.

I felt that the Online Journal was a very effective way to record and respond to the ideas we were learning in class. I participated in all of the assigned journal entries, even if some of them were brief. In this way, I kept up with the pace of the course and accumulation of information.

I am fairly pleased with the progress of my final report. Since writing papers is not my favorite activity, I am struggling to complete this one at the last minute, and could have done a little better if I had started it sooner. However, this was a challenging semester for me and I’m satisfied with my achievements so far.

I find it fascinating that the field of archaeology has scientists who can discern fairly detailed facts about human activity at a site from the debitage found there, for example, whether the person was left or right-handed or what stage of production they were in when they stopped working on their tool. These and other insights into human behavior will get more detailed as technology gets more precise and I think this is valuable information about various cultures.

I’ve also always been intrigued by the Mayan culture, especially the extraordinary symbolism in their artworks and architecture. I would enjoyed studying the culture more and could justify this endeavor in that it inspires me in my field. On plenty of occasions, lectures spawned ideas for further exploration, which I took note of.
One of the Lab Deliverables I enjoyed most was the analyzing of the codex. I think more time could be spent on this area of the course as it is a remarkable representation of valuable archaeological findings.

Another Lab Deliverable prominent in my recollection is the Divine Mission Video. This video covered the technical and social processes involved in the restoration of a historical site. The conservation principles of minimal intervention and respecting the integrity of a work of art by not putting too much of yourself into the work are important guidelines to follow if we are to honor the intent of the work to be a reflection of human lives.

My primary strengths with this course have been my commitment to attend all the class meetings and labs and keep up with the assignments along the way. I require improvement in the area of final reports, which have always been a source of anxiety for me. However, I made some progress in this area, even though I went over the deadline.
