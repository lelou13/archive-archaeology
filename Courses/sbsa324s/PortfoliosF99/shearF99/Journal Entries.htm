Author: Ashley Shear.- Subject: Chichen

Date: 9/22/99.- Time: 1:00:23 AM.- Entry Num: 234

Chichen Itza was first discovered during the speculative period when people just guessed what was there without really looking into the site. From there people during the descriptive period went a little deeper and looked a little harder to find the answers. Augustus Le Plongeon found the site in the 1800's and found Chac Mool, which means Reclining Man of worrier. He chopped away the vegetation to find more. During the Historical period is when scholars from Harvard when to the palaces to find the treasures of the kings. They didn't look around the surrounding area, just at the obvious. After 1940 which is the explanatory period is when archaeologists began to look outside the obvious and look to the outskirts where there is just as much information. Archaeologists found that it was the Maya who had lived there and the archaeologist put together how the Maya lived. In the slides it showed how the city was laid out. Massive structures adorned with paintings and symbols that explain the history of these peoples. How the Maya's lived and how they sacrificed people. The slides showed the massive structures with the serpents and the gods in them. There is so much to discover about the Maya and how they lived.

Author: Ashley Shear.- Subject: Virtual Dig 1

Date: 11/10/99.- Time: 1:06:33 AM.- Entry Num: 137

The virtual dig helped me understand what kind of planning goes into a site that needs to be excavated. Figuring out how many people are needed for the dig and what kind of accommodations are needed helped me see what goes into a site. When we first submitted our proposal it was rejected because we didn't have enough people to dig and we didn't have the right specialists. When we went back in and changed some things around we got our proposal accepted although it wasn't perfect. The factors that I see that are most important to a dig is the number of people you have according to the number of excavation sites. There should be at least one person per site otherwise you will not get the job done. The other thing that I saw by doing this virtual dig is that you should pay the airfare for all the people who will be working on the site. That way if there isn't a person in the area who can do the job you can have someone flown into the site to do the job. Another thing I noticed is that you should have specialists according to the history of the site. If it is know that the site is really old you should have a person who can tell the difference between new and old humans. With this site we knew that we would probably find a lot of stone tools so we had a stone tool specialists. I found that when making a budget you should include all specialists and you should pay for everyone's airfare.

Author: Ashley Shear.- Subject: LD: Divine mission Video

Date: 11/3/99.- Time: 1:13:11 AM.- Entry Num: 109

San Xavier del Bac is located on 14 acres in Tucson, Arizona. The mission was built by the Franciscan Priests of the area. The mission has been slowly deteriorating. The community surrounding the mission pulled together some funds and started to restore the mission to its original brilliance. Over the years the mission had only been repaired where needed. They repaired it with cement on the outside and on the inside they just painted over the paintings and many of them were gone. The community came together and started to repair the mission using natural materials that were used when the mission was built. They took the cement off of the building and replaced it with a natural mortar, made of cactus juice and other materials. On the inside they took all the layers of paint off to find their original paintings that were there. When they were finished the mission was restored to the natural brilliance and they left caretakers behind so the mission doesn't fall in to disrepair again.

Author: Ashley Shear.- Subject: Garbology 1

Date: 11/17/99.- Time: 1:03:48 AM.- Entry Num: 155

Today in class we watched a the CNN special called "Food to Die For" which was about garbology. The University of Colorado garbology class studied various families and found that most families basically lied about what they were consuming. One particular student found that while the family he was studying said they were eating healthy foods, they were in actuality eating poorly. The student found a lot of fast food wrappers and other quick foods. We also looked at another case study about a couple that ran a day care. The study looked at the consumption of liquids. In the beginning the couple was drinking a lot of beer and Pepsi and there was also a lot of CapriSun. When the researcher confronted the couple about the consumption of alcohol, the next week it dropped dramatically. We looked at one more case study which was done on a male. From his garbage you could tell that he had had a cold was preparing for a date and that he didn't like to cook. From these three examples of garbology studies it seems to me that you can tell a lot about a person from their garbage. By studying a persons trash you can tell what there diet it, you can tell if they are sick or if they have just cleaned. You can tell what there lifestyle is like. A person who eats a lot of fast food is most likely on the run and very busy with no time to make a decent meal. Most people probably think they are eating right when in actuality they aren't eating very well. Everyone should go through their garbage to see how they are really eating.
