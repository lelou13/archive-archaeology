Lab Deliverable 3: Material Cultures Assessment

Material Culture Activity 1:

Having found a chert core for flaking stone tools from an archaeological site, this would make me believe that the people who lived on the land knew how to build a fire and used this tool regularly to make a fire. The Spanish colonial trade bead may be an artifact that was either a trade with a Spaniard, or a Spaniard was living on the land. The Borine tooth may indicate what kind of beef was eaten on the area, or hunted. This may also indicate that those who lived in the area were carnivores.

Material Culture Activity 2:

In viewing the codex, the first thought that comes to mind is that the ancient Mexicans who created this codex were of a very rich culture who chose to record specific events in their lives, in addition, had the capability to formulate their own type of written communication through pictures, symbols, colors and counts. I believe that the codex that was shown was interpreted as a type of life cycle diagram that illustrated the lives of these ancient Mexican People, the Seasons and how they lived during those times, their riches, their beliefs and cultures. The codex gives full description of their lives. This is how I feel the manuscript was interpreted and "read." The specific section of the manuscript that I have chosen is a scene where rich architecture is displayed. An ancient Mexican, perhaps somebody of high status because of the elaborate hair dress and costume, is inside a perfectly drawn circle decorated with intricate curls all around on the outside of the circle. This shows that these people had already discovered geometric shapes and perhaps not only applied it through their drawings, codex, but also with their architecture in their village, or home. Also part of the scene is an Eagle riding on some type of carriage. This would depict that the Eagle was a very sacred symbol. The other two images on this manuscript is of two individuals richly costumed standing atop a platform, a platform with very detailed carvings. This may show that these individuals were very important and played important roles in the survival of the village therefore stood "above" amongst the rest of the people. All in all, the primary theme in this manuscript, I believe, is giving ode to the noble peoples or symbols (the eagle) that these Ancient Peoples gave credit to. A few related items on the codex are the beads with the different colors, these may all represent a type of count of something. Another are the animals that stand beside some individuals, these are the names as stated by the professor. Another is tree looking symbols, family trees I believe is what these are.

Material Culture Activity 3:

Amongst the artifacts handed to us for observation, I think that out of 6, 3 of them were definite arrowheads, or tools used for hunting. Artifact 4.7 was shaped trianguly with sharp edges and a fairly sharp point about an inch long and its highest width at about 3/4 an inch. Opposite the sharp point was a straight edge where the artifact must have once been attached to an arrow or fishing line. Artifact 1.12 and 4.42 resembled 4.7 largely. Artifact 4.42 is perhaps an arrowhead used for long and high aims because its length is much greater than those of the other two and is much narrower in width, while being very light weight, perhaps 1 1/2 inches long and 1/4 inch wide. In comparison, artifact 1.12 is the smallest of the arrowheads I observed standing about the size of my thumbnail, perhaps a little bit bigger. The little arrowhead was perhaps used for mainly fishing lines because of its size but fairly sharp point and edges. Similar to the other two, it too was fairly smooth all around and lightweight. One artifact that was tremendously different from the rest of the artifacts I observed was number 4.37. This artifact was a possible carving and cutting tool used for maintaining and sharpening other rock tools. This rock appears to be very sturdy and sharp, while having much smoother surfaces than the other stone tools. The color is a blackish coffee color with a little transparency that gives it a very rich look. Perhaps this is an obsidian rock. I think this was not used for hunting because it is a little bit heavier than the other stone tools and would not hold on an arrow. In addition, I think this stone tool served much more than one purpose.

Material Culture Activity 4:

My artifact is 4.30 with a Munsell Color of N2-0 (Grayish Black). In the Munsell color chart it is being measured differently from the other colors, where it is being measured by how gray the color is. It looks to be a chert stone tool, lightweight with sharp edges.

Material Culture Activity 4b:

The cortex for Artifact 4.30 is primary in means of classification for the flake type: complete, early-stage biface thinning. The two faces have some parallelism showing no ridges or other surfaces besides the face and back. I believe this artifact is mostly all of its own cortex even after the flaking. This artifact may have had some pressure flaking because the edges are quite sharp and defined. I do see a few flake scars on one side of the artifact. I believe that the side with the least flake scars is the exterior (dorsal) of the artifact, while the other side with the most flake scars, is the interior (ventral) of the artifact because it is the percussion platform
