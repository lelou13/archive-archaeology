Excavation Day 4

On the afternoon of my fourth trip to the Mission, I continued working on the same site and level, N10W38NE1/4 Level 2.  The goal for the afternoon was to reach level 3 at 40 centimeters. On the way, quite a lot of artifacts were found. Similar to the morning, in the afternnon another 1,000g of floor tile was found in the site. What was more amazing was that as we got deeper in the site, many more interesting artifacts began to appear. THe first was a beautiful ceramic piece found at N56cm W35cm at D40cm, and weighing about 43.7 g. It appeared to be Brunido ware, perhaps originating from the English about 150 or so years ago, according to Chris, Dr. Mendoza's assistant. The piece contained artistry that is believed to originate from the English, and of an image, sort of like a lighthouse or house. Later on in the dig, two more ceramic pieces were found at level 2 at 37.5 cm and 37 cm deep. The two pieces, similar to the first, appeared to originate from England or France. Those were the most interesting and exciting findings for the day on the site I worked on. By the end of the day, we did manage to take N10W38NE1/4 to the 3rd leve, at 40 cm.
