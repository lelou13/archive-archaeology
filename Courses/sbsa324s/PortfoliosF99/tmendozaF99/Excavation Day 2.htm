Excavation Day 2


In my second trip out to the Old San Juan Bautista Mission, I pursued to dig right about the same area except in the N10, W38, SE1/4 region. My findings were quite succesful consisting of about 857 g of tile, bone fragments, glass, silt stone, stucco, stone, shell, metal and brick fragments. The large amount of tile that was found in that region may be an indication of a possible foundation in that area, where a wall might have also been present or sometime of landscaping. The bone fragments could be of chicken bones. The glass was of opalized glass. The metal, possibly some type of a corroded metal fragments. Although I was more familiar with digging this time, such as measuring my area's height and using a pick ax much more efficiently, I still could not help notice the hard work that really goes into archaeological digs. The findings you find are the rewards of such hard labor, but it sure would be devastating to a group who found nothing but dirt. However, finding nothing but dirt might be a step
