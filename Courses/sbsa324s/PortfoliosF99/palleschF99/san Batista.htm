I accidently posted this to the class journal for my morning class. This was for 9/17/99 This was my first trip out to the mission last friday. This trip was a whole new experience to me and I picked up a few new things. I grouped up with Ashley, and she gave me the run down on what pretty much to do. I did most of the digging that day while she sifted and looked for artifacts. After a few hours of digging my hands were taking tole to the work. I can see how this job is tough. Overall we found mostly rocks, but we did come across some bone, glass, and white wall fragments. In addition to those we found a small peice of ceramic/pottery that had painting on it. One of the other excavaters there told us it was probably from a piece of English pottery. I await this next friday to find some more treasures

This was the day to remember, a full day of digging out at San Juan. Pretty much I got out there and me and another fellow dug and dug and dug all day long. We went from around 30cm deep to about 62 deep. We were working in the N10 W38 SW corner. We took about a 30 minute lunch break, (had supreme hamburgers).. We really didn't discover anything great, other than the usual findings. But, it was definitly the gossip and technology day. We had some really good conversations and I think came together as a good group of workers that day. So signing off from archaeology.. See ya in Y2K

 

 

Author: Mike Palleschi.- Subject: San Juan Batista Thoughts.- 14 of 16Records Found.

Date: 10/19/99.- Time: 11:40:06 PM.- Entry Num: 72

Just a few thoughts on the dig. As a first time archaeologist digger, I had no clue what kind of work it took. With the sore muscles, blisters, and dirty clothes I kind of have grasped it. And, its not all that exciting digging there, but when you think you have found something you get that feeling of joy and a smile arises on your face. I have really enjoyed the experience so far, I will continue to attend at least 3-4 more visits to have a total of around 6-7. Oh, to all those people who have never dug through adobe, you are really missing out! Some of the things that my group has discovered are; tile fragments, iron nails/spikes, glass, potterey fragmetns, foundation fragments, bones, and a 1972 copper penny.
