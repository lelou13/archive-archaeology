Major Deliverable 3: Lab & Field Journal

Introduction:  The Field Journal represents that work that you have done to date in class and in the field portion of the excavation or lab project.  As such, the journal should reflect your ability to accurately, clearly, and meticulously document what you have observed, experienced, or studied to this point in the semester.

    Online Journal entries will be used to document Lab Deliverables (be they in the Archaeology Lab or at the excavation site) and field days.  As such, these should reflect an accurate consideration of the content of that particular day(s) lab study, or field assignment.  Where no journal entry has been posted to the Online Journal, you should have documentation, in the form of notes or e-mail, that provides a substitute form for documentation and technical descriptions pertaining to that particular lab or field deliverable.

1.    In using the Online Journal to search for your entries, simply enter your full first and last name into the Name Search on the left side of the screen.  Once you have entered your name, simply click on the search button, and the Online Journal should recover all of your journal entries to date.

2.    If you are using an e-mail backup of the Online Journal, simply cut and paste the content of your e-mail messages into a Microsoft Word or Corel WordPerfect document and save the new document in the following form:  [course number, last name, semester, and Major Deliverable number].  Professor Mendoza's journal would follow the following format in terms of file name:  sbsa324sMendozaF99MD1].

3.    Using the Online Journal, or your related document or e-mail files of the same, prepare a print version of all of your Online Journal entries to date and submit to Professor Mendoza by the next class meeting.

4.    Professor Mendoza will assess the content of your Online Journal entries in terms of readability, content, clarity, critical thinking and reflection, engagement with the subject-matter, lab and field participation, and the appropriate use of key terms and concepts from the field of archaeology.
