Lab Deliverable 3: Material Cultures Assessment
Instructions:  Drawing on those items provided by your professor, you will undertake two distinct tasks.  The first task will entail interpreting images or items of material culture from an item of "foreign" manufacture.  In this instance, you will undertake to provide a "technical description" of the body of material culture represented.  In the second instance, you will each be provided with some item of material culture, and you will take on the challenge of describing said item in a second clearly crafted and concise "technical description."
    By technical description, I am referring to a description that provides a clear, concise, and technically accurate and precise description of some artifact or process.  For example, if you were to describe the computer on your desktop, you would provide a description of the size, shape, color, composition, function, and origins of the item of material culture in question.

1.    In this first instance, you will break into groups and examine the facsimile copy of the ancient Mexican screen-fold "codex" brought to lab by your professor.  In examing the "codex" in question, you are to interpret the following:

    a.    First, identify and describe the primary function and overall format of the codex in question.
    b.    Second, provide an interpretation of how you believe the manuscript in question was interpreted and "read."
    c.    Third, identify the primary theme of that section of the manuscript that you and your group have chosen.
    d.    Select at least a half dozen items of material culture and describe them as a group of "related" items.

2.    The second exercise in this lab entails creating a technical description for the artifacts provided by your professor.  You are to provide a technical description that includes the "Who, What, Where, When, Why, and How" of the item in question.  In this instance, you should interpret this to mean the "size, shape, color, composition, function, and origins" of the artifact in question.  Use that terminology that you believe to be appropriate in this instance.
