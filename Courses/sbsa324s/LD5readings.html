Lab Deliverable 5: Readings Review
Instructions:  Drawing on the text by David Hurst Thomas (Archaeology: Down to Earth), and one other readings resource, you are to produce an essay summary that serves to answer the following questions.  Please be advised that you are required to draw directly on the readings cited (specifically Chapters 1, 2, and 5) in David Hurst Thomas in preparing your answers.

In addition, you are to integrate into your narrative reflections on your personal participation and observations in the lab and field exercises undertaken this semester.  Due Date: Thursday, December 17th at 5:00pm (Office: Building 17, Room 117).

1.    How does David Hurst Thomas define the scope and objectives of the discipline of archaeology?  Given his definition, how do your preconceptions (preconceived notions) about archaeology and archaeologists differ with the reality defined and discussed by Thomas?

2.    Why do "archaeologists walk straight lines and dig square holes" according to David Hurst Thomas?  How does the approach taken by David Hurst Thomas in his excavations of the Spanish missions of Florida differ from what you yourself have experienced by virtue of the excavation at Old Mission San Juan Bautista?

3.    Based on David Hurst Thomas' explanation and description of "Anthropology, Science, and the Humanities", how would you define the "anthropological approach" to archaeology and culture?  In addition, please define the concept of culture in so far as your response to the definition of the anthropological approach to archaeology.  In other words, what is "culture" according to David Hurst Thomas?

4.    Finally, what is the scientific method, and how is said method used by archaeologists in order to conduct lab and field research?  Be sure to define the scientific method, and how said method is utilized in the field of archaeology!
