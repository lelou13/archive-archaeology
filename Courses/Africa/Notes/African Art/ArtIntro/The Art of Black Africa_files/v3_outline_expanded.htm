Introducing Africa
Reference: Frank Willett (1993) “African Art.”
Africa Art: Diversity reduced to homogeneity and generalizations in art texts.
Africa Described: 12 million sq. miles (3xUS land area), 500 million + population; narrow coastal plains = minimal external contact.
Environment: Presents limitations to art and culture…does not determine either.  From desert to savanna to forest to montane to mediterranean to savanna mosaics.

African Environments
Ecology: What role does the environment play in the art and culture of Africa? (e.g., Chi wara headress = double antelope)
Change: What types of environmental changes have affected Africa? (e.g., eastern Niger 1400 BCE or Sahara pre-2000 BCE)
Peoples: How have the peoples and cultures of Africa changed? (e.g., Bushmen and “Old Stone Age”)

The Art of Migration
Migration: How does a migratory lifestyle affect the nature and character of that art and architecture produced?
Rock Art: What types of cultures are most likely to produce such art?
Ephemeral vs Enduring Arts: Music, dance, poetry, narration (griot), tattoo, basketry, painting, sculpture = pastoral economies vs urban centers.

Limitations on Production
Wood Sculpture: What sorts of factors inhibit the character of artistic productions crafted from wood? (e.g., masks)

Forest Art: Subtractive vs additive styles (e.g., sculpture vs plastic arts; Nok of Nigeria)
