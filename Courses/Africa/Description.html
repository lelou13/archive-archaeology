Course Description


Lost Civilizations of Africa and Eurasia reviews the rise and fall of ancient states and civilizations in Africa, Egypt and the Mediterranean. Primary emphasis is with the reading and writing of archaeology and history as this pertains to the civilizations of sub-Saharan Africa and Eurasia, and to a lesser extent Europe.

This learning experience will review the comparative development and contributions of those ancient cultures and civilizations identified with regions as diverse as ancient Egypt, Sub-Saharan Africa, and the Mediterranean. Because this learning experience is comparative in scope, such ancient cultures, kingdoms and states as those of dynastic Egypt, Axum, Meroe, Zimbabwe, Benin, Kush, and the East African kingdoms serve as the primary case studies for this exploration into the archaeology of complex societies. The comparative approach of this learning experience will rely on materials ranging from internet sites and CD-rom resources through readings, videos, lectures and problem-oriented discussions and overviews that serve to frame the rise of civilization in Africa and Eurasia.

Special information: SBSD 383 is the upper division counterpart of SBSD 283. It is recommended that students whose SBSC Area 3 Specialized Competency lies in the area(s) of archaeology, cultural studies, or museum education complete this course, as well as its complement, Lost Civilizations of the Pacific Rim.

This offering addresses (in part) SBSC Major Learning Outcomes: Area 1: Theory, Area 2: Information Management, Data Retrieval and Analysis, and Area 3: Specialized Competencies (archaeology, cultural studies, museum education); and CSUMB University Learning Requirements for Creative/Artistic Expression.  Upper division credit for this course is available by registering for SBSD 383. Upper division credit will require additional assignments, projects, and or readings appropriate to an upper division course.
