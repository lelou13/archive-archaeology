Faculty need only review recent campus technology initiatives to see that a considerable degree of technical and scholarly attention has been devoted to the design and institutionalization of distributed learning models and environments targeting the promotion of virtual student enrollment or e-FTE. Despite this fact, little has been done to promote the development of new user-friendly models and approaches to facilitated learning, or faculty-friendly and student-centered instructional technology applications and courseware for the enhancement of hands-on (lab and field) and classroom-based instruction. This demonstration will introduce participants to the use of online and virtual tools for the design of effective approaches to the enhancement of facilitated learning for the culture and language-oriented classroom environment.

This Internet Resource Collection was prepared for a presentation at the 2nd Annual "Emerging Technologies in Teaching Languages and Culture" conference held at the California State University, Monterey Bay, on March 23rd, 2000.  A slide show of the presentation is available by clicking here.

So that participants may reflect on the conference experience, or on the presentation topic regarding the "Virtual Learning Lab: Facilitated Teaching and Learning in the Online Environment," you may post your comments here.  Please feel free to ask questions or suggest additional links to this page of Internet resources pertaining to teaching and learning language and culture in the online environment.
