Latin@ Link: Web Sites of the Latina and Latino Internet
Posted as of March 9, 1998
Compiled by Ruben G. Mendoza, Ph.D.

The following sites have been selected so as to provide some initial leads for browsing the World Wide Web in search of Latina and Latino-related web resources in and beyond the social sciences. It should be noted that while these links are provided as a service to the ALLA membership, the posting of said Internet links should in no way be regarded as an endorsement for products or viewpoints touted within the context of the links included here.

Chicano Counterculture


BrownPride Online: Art and History

http://www.brownpride.com/arte.html

Created by Digital Aztlan, a Latino media company started by three computer science students from CSU Fullerton, this site features everything from Lowriders and Zuit Suiters to Chicano murals. The "Art and History" section is notable for its images of California's Chicano muralism. The graphics and content are far ranging and creative, and this site provides an overview of Chicano history from the perspective of Chicano youth. Unlike other sites listed below, this site does contain some commerical endorsements and takes some liberties targeting the youth market.


Online Guide to Gangs Signs and Graffiti

http://www.courses.edu/gang-guide.html

An Internet Guide to Gang Signs and Behavior for a course on Gang Recognition and Behavior offered through the University of the Pacific Enhancement Course Program.


Pano: Art From the Inside Out/Museum of International Folk Art

http://www.state.nm.us/MOIFAOnLine/Panos/

Internet site of the Museum of International Folk Art pertaining to an exhibition on Latino street art of the counterculture.

Indigenous Mexico

Zapatistas In Cyberspace: A Guide to Analysis and Resources

http://www.eco.utexas.edu/Homepages/Faculty/Cleaver/zapsincyber.html#Analyses

A detailed summary and extensive listing of resources currently on the Internet regarding the Zapatista Liberation Army's rebellion in Chiapas. The author contends that the Zapatista's and their supporters have launched the first successful global information campaign ever waged on the Internet. Based on John Holloway (ed.), The Chiapas Uprising and the Future of Revolution in the Twenty-First Century, 1998?.


Instituto Nacional Indigenista

http://serpiente.dgsca.unam.mx/ini/

The beginnings of what promises to be an excellent resource on the projects and peoples served by Mexico's Instituto Nacional Indigenista. At present, in addition to an overview of the INI programs mission statement and history, the site currently contains a statistical breakdown of census information on the indigenous peoples of the states of Chihuahua and Baja California, Mexico.

International Migration Studies


Centre for Research in International Migration and Ethnic Relations

http://www.ceifo.su.se/

Provides a forum for the study and publication of scholarly papers concerned with international migration and ethnic relations. Papers currently posted include treatment of the question of international migration in Uruguay and related Latin American countries.

Language Services


Alta Vista Translation Service

http://babelfish.altavista.digital.com/cgi-bin/translate?

Provides for the automatic on-line translation of text from English to Spanish, Spanish to English, and from or to French, German, and Italian.

Latin American Studies


H-LatAm

http://h-net2.msu.edu/~latam/

An on-line journal of papers and related materials pertaining to Latin American history. Site maintained by H-Net Humanities and Social Sciences OnLine of the Michigan State University.


LatAm Internet Links

http://h-net2.msu.edu/~latam/links/

A subsection of the H-LatAm site that hosts a variety of Web links to other sites of interests to Latin Americanists.


UT-LANIC Joint Projects and Hosted Databases, Institute of Latin American Studies, University of Texas at Austin.

http://lanic.utexas.edu/

An excellent resource on Latin America and Latin American studies. Includes template for searching for resources by country and topic.

Latina and Latino Internet Directories


Directorio Global Net

http://www.dirglobal.net/

Provides a key word search and awesome listing of Internet links of interest or concern to Latinas and Latinos. This site is formatted to call on the linked resources in Spanish that currently exist on the WWW.

Mexican Colonial


Conversos Judasaintes Tried by the Méxican Inquisition, 1528-1815

http://www.startext.net/homes/longoria/Jews2.htm#This

An annotated listing of the names of individuals tried as Jews by the Holy Office of the Inquisition in Mexico. The listing is alphabetical, and allows for the search of specific classes of people tried by the Holy Office.

Mexican Internet


FUNTEC: Internet en Mexico

http://www.funtec.org/mexico.html

A study of the role and impact of the internet in Mexico with links that provide information on current internet providers and the extent to which Mexico is developing infrastructure for the buildout of the Information Superhighway.


Network Information Center - Mexico: Historia del Internet en Mexico

http://www.nic.mx/evol/historia.html

Begins with an overview of the prehistory of the Internet in Mexico (ca. 1989) and precedes in detailed fashion to document the many infrastructural and technological changes that have served to network Mexico and its academic and business interests.

Pre-Hispanic Mexico


The Mayan Epigraphic Database Project (MED)

http://jefferson.village.virginia.edu/med/medwww.html

An experiment in networked scholarship developed with the purpose of enhancing Classic Mayan epigraphic research. Housed at the Institute of Advanced Technology in the Humanities at the University of Virginia, this site was originally developed by Raf Alvarado, a graduate student in anthropology at the University of Virginia. This site may well prove to be one of the most revolutionary new resources for the collaborative study of the ancient Maya glyph system.


Tarlton Law Library Resources on Aztec and Maya Law

http://www.law.utexas.edu/rare/aztec.htm

An annotated bibliography on resources pertaining to the study of Aztec and Maya law at the University of Texas.

Puerto Rico


La Genealogia de Puerto Rico: The Genealogy of Puerto Rico

http://www.rootsweb.com/~prwgw/

An excellent resource for the study of Puerto Riqueno genealogy and family history. Includes an extensive clickable database of names and towns, and related links on Puerto Rico and the Puerto Riquenos.

Southwestern Missions


National Park Service: San Antonio Missions National Historic Park

http://www.nps.gov/saan/saan.htm

An excellent overview of the Old Spanish missions of the area of San Antonio, Texas, including San Antonio de Valero, or the "Alamo." Take the guided tour by way of the "Knowledge Trail."


The Historic Missions of West Texas

http://www.cibola.net/missions/index.html

Spain


Internet y la Historia de Espana

http://h-net2.msu.edu/~latam/links/spanish.html

An excellent overview, in Spanish, of internet resources for the study of the history of Spain. Site maintained by H-Net Humanities and Social Sciences OnLine of the Michigan State University.

Spanish Colonial Art and Culture


Spanish Colonial Arts Society

http://www.spanishcolonial.org/

http://www.spanishcolonial.org/arts.html

The arts.html portion of this site provides an overview of Spanish colonial arts ranging from Santos to metal works.


Museum of International Folk Art, Santa Fe: Spanish Colonial Art

http://www.state.nm.us/MOIFAOnLine/PermanentCollections/spanishcolonial.html

A nicely illustrated overview of Spanish Colonial and contemporary Hispanic folk art.
