The Historic Resources Collection

While much of the documents collection of Old Mission San Juan Bautista has been dispersed or lost as the result of deterioration, mishandling, and removal or sale by collectors, there remains a significant collection of early documents and photographs in the archives of the Old Mission.  While the Old Mission's manuscript collection consists of 17th through early 20th century books and papers, and a few early handwritten letters and notes, much of the original early document collection now resides at the Santa Barbara Mission Archive, the Bancroft Library, and in a variety of other public and private repositories and collections.  For those using the CD-ROM, this section is intended to provide a password-protected online repository for those interested in reviewing transcriptions of early documents and copies of digitized versions of those documents available to this project.  Please return to this portion of the CD-ROM's linked collections of resources for future installments and additions to the historic resources section.
Los Ynformes: The Annual Reports

The "Ynformes" or "Informes" represent the annual reports made by the early Franciscan missionaries of Old Mission San Juan Bautista.  These reports span the years from 1797 through 1840, and represent the annual accounting related to population figures (birthm marriage, and mortality), religious and civil ceremonial activity, agricultural activity and productivity, construction activity, and both episodes of prosperity and decline.

Facsimile Versions of Early Documents

The facsimiles or digital versions of early documents found at this site are intended for your perusal.  While most of the earliest documents are written entirely in Spanish, and as such, should prove beneficial to those who read the Spanish language, the often cryptic and very elaborate style of writing used in these early documents may prove more than most can handle for the purposes of interpretation or transcription.  Nevertheless, the document samples provided here should enlighten the CD-ROM user to the wealth of information and documentary evidence that remains largely undeciphered or unstudied in many an historical repository.

Early Narratives

This section of the site is intended to provide extended quotations and commentaries from travel narratives, personal accounts, and descriptions of the community of San Juan Bautista.  Much of this material has been compiled from transcripts, printed documents, and other early print resources available at the Old Mission or community of San Juan Bautista.
