3D Modeling and Animation
The reconstruction of archaeological sites and artifact finds has traditionally proceeded from interpretation through illustration, or, by way of the actual physical reconstruction or modeling of the monument or artifact in question.  Today, the availability of computer-based 3D (three dimensional) modeling and animation programs has enabled archaeologists to move well beyond the static models and renderings of yesteryear.  The 3D examples or electronic mappings provided in this section will provide one basis for assessing the utility of such programs in approaching the "reconstruction" and interpretation of your site or artifact study.
