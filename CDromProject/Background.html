Project Origins and Objectives

Digital Photo by Cara Davis.
During the summer of 2000, Institute Director Ruben Mendoza and his students first undertook, and completed within six weeks, the development of an instructional CD-ROM aimed at illuminating the archaeology, art, and history of the Old Mission and community of San Juan Bautista, California.  Because Dr. Mendoza's archaeological and ethnohistorical research in said community began in the summer of 1995, Dr. Mendoza has amassed thousands of photographic images of the community, including events ranging from the yearly Fiesta to the Old Mission's bicentennial celebration.  And that in addition to the many images devoted to documenting the archaeological and historical investigations completed thus far.  In addition, because his excavations and museum education project efforts -- spanning the past five years -- have been recorded on S-VHS tape, as well as in digital media, audio, hardcopy documents, and electronic maps, it was time to convert a small fraction of that media to a medium that would benefit the community and its many visitors.
See other examples of Cenan Pirani's image editing by clicking on the image to the left.

 
In the Spring of 2000, Dr. Mendoza submitted a research proposal to the CSU Research, Scholarship, and Creative Activity Awards Competition at CSU Monterey Bay.  As a result of his submission -- titled "Archaeology, Multimedia and Online Resources for Old Mission San Juan Bautista" -- Dr. Mendoza was awarded $5,000.00 to undertake the hiring of six student assistants to produce an instructional courseware project intended to support Professor Mendoza's many teaching and research-related endeavors.  Because little time or resources were available for some of the preliminary planning and expertise necessary for the undertaking in question, the assembled team found innovative solutions and undertook a considerable amount of problem-solving to bring the product to fruition.  Some of the challenges and innovations undertaken will be documented in future installments on this portion of the site.
