Historic San Juan Bautista

Bring history to life and the local community into your classroom as you step back through time in historic San Juan Bautista. Explore the town’s authentic Spanish Colonial Mission, investigate maps and manuscripts, and participate in an archaeological excavation along side Dr. Ruben Mendoza, Director of the Institute of Archaeology at California State University, Monterey Bay.

"Do history," rather than read history as you learn first hand how historical archaeologists unlock secrets of the past. Examine artifacts from the lives of American Indians, Spanish padres, Mexican craftsman, and American homesteaders. Using digital cameras and scanners capture images of California’s rich past for use in classroom presentations or as resources for your students’ own projects and investigations. Create maps and timelines with easy to use software appropriate for students of almost any age.

After two days in "the town that time passed by," you’ll view your own community as a rich resource for social studies, language arts, and even math curricula. A special emphasis will be placed on community studies for 3rd grade teachers, California history, and archaeological techniques appropriate for middle school students.
