California K12 Education Standards
The following K12 Education standards were abstracted by Liz Talbot from the host of California state standards to be found at www.cde.ca.gov/cilbranch/eltdiv/cdsmc.html or http://www.cde.ca.gov/statetests/history/history.html This is not a comprehensive list of those standards that are applicable to the venue at San Juan Bautista.  This list is intended strictly as a guide to get you started with potential themes and curriculum targets for the classroom.

Grades Four - Eight:  History Social Science

In addition to the standards, students demonstrate the following intellectual, reasoning, reflection and research skills:
  A. Chronological and Spatial Thinking
  B. Research, Evidence, and Point of View
  C. Historical Interpretation

Grade Four:  History-Social Science

    1.5 Students describe the social, political, cultural, and economic life and interactions among people of California from the pre-Columbian societies to the Spanish mission and Mexican rancho periods.

Grade Five:  History-Social Science

    1.5 Students describe the cooperation and conflict that existed among the American Indians and between the Indian nations and the new settlers.

    1.5 Students trace the colonization, immigration, and settlement patterns of the American people from 1789 to the mid-1800s, with emphasis on the role of economic incentives, effects of the physical and political geography, and transportation systems.

Grade Six:  History-Social Science

    1.5 Students describe what is known through archaeological studies of the early physical and cultural development of humankind from the Paleolithic era to the agricultural revolution.

Grade Seven:  History-Social Science

Connecting with Past Learnings
-Uncovering the Remote Past.
-How do we know about the past?
-The work of archeologists and historians.

Grade Eight:  History-Social Science

The Divergent Paths of the American People: 1800-1850/The West
    1.5 Students analyze the aspirations and ideals of the people of the new nation.
    1.6 Students analyze U.S. foreign policy in the early Republic.
    1.7 Students analyze the divergent paths of the American people in the West from 1800 to the mid-1800s and the challenges they faced.

Grade Four: Science

Investigation and Experimentation
    6. Scientific progress is made by asking meaningful questions and conducting careful investigations.

Grade Five: Science

Investigation and Experimentation
    6. Scientific progress is made by asking meaningful questions and conducting careful investigations. 

Grade Six: Science

Plate Tectonics and Earth’s Structure.
    Investigation and Experimentation (e.g., stratigraphy & uniformitarianism)

Grade Seven: Science

Earth and Life History
4. Evidence from rocks allows us to understand the evolution of life on Earth.
    Investigation and Experimentation.

Grade Four: Language Arts

Reading Comprehension
    1.5 Compare and contrast information on the same topic after reading several passages or articles
    1.6 Distinguish between cause and effect and fact and opinion in expository text
    1.7 Follow multiple-step instructions from a basic technical manual (e.g. Computer)

Writing
    1.5 Select focus, organization, and point of view based upon purpose, audience, length and format requirements.
    1.6 Create a multiple paragraph composition

Research and Technology
    1.5 quote or paraphrase information sources, citing them appropriately
    1.7 use various reference materials
    1.9 demonstrate basic keyboarding skills and familiarity with the vocabulary of technology
